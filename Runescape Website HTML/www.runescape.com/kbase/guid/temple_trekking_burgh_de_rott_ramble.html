Temple Trekking / Burgh de Rott Ramble

Temple Trekking | Burgh de Rott Ramble | Location | Requirements | Recommended Items
Making the Journey | Aggressive Events | Obstacle Events | Rewards | Development Team


Temple Trekking and Burgh de Rott Ramble are only accessible by RuneScape Members. Please subscribe to get this feature.

Please note this is a 'Dangerous' activity. If you die during this activity, you will lose your items.

Temple Trekking

Burgh de Rott refugees
A group of villagers wishes to leave Burgh de Rott and escape to Misthalin, via the Temple of Saradomin also known as Paterdomus. The three routes leading to the Temple consist of various adventures, challenges and puzzles. Only the brave shall walk these routes to help the villagers reach their destination. Will you help?

Getting to the Temple is no easy task. With a choice of three routes from easy to hard and six different people to choose from, varying from the weak to the strong, which route will you take to get to the Temple?

Burgh de Rott Ramble

Mercenaries at Paterdomus
With the discovery of Burgh de Rott and the misery of its citizens, adventurers from across the world are flooding the north-west border of Morytania, all waiting eagerly to aid the poor villagers in their plight. Unfortunately, none of them know the way, but they're willing to pay handsomely for a guide...

Location

Map of Mort Myre Swamp
To begin your journey on Burgh de Rott Temple Trek, you must be able to pass into Morytania and hence must have completed the Priest In Peril quest. When you have done this quest, you can access Morytania which includes the town of Canifis from where you can take the route to Burgh de Rott.

When you are ready to leave, head west out of Canifis and then south-west until you reach the mushroom patch. From this patch you should see a gate, which you can pass through if you have completed or started the Nature Spirit quest.

From here, it is highly recommended you set 'Run' to 'On' and run south as fast as possible until you reach a wooden bridge. Running is recommended, as Mort Myre is plagued by ghasts, which are known to rot food and you will need these for your trek!

From the bottom of the Myre, follow the winding path along towards the town of Mort'ton and head south towards Burgh de Rott.

Once you have completed Darkness of Hallowvale you may elect not to bother with this route, and instead escort an adventurer south before returning with a villager.
Normal route
Myreque shortcut


Requirements

There are no skill requirements, but the following quests are required:
  • Priest In Peril
  • Nature Spirit
  • In Search Of The Myreque
  • In Aid of the Myreque


To begin Burgh de Rott Ramble, you must also have completed Darkness of Hallowvale.

Recommended Items

Important: Please note that there is a risk of death, so you should only bring items/weapons that you are willing to lose.

The routes to the Temple can be quite dangerous, with some tough enemies to fight on the way, so why not take some good armour and fill your inventory with food? You can give your refugee/mercenary food before you set out on your journey, which will bring up a nice interface to deal with, rather then trying to feed them while ghasts and vampyres are trying to feed on you!

The following items are recommended along with the strong armour and food to help overcome the challenges:

Ivandis Flail or the Rod of Ivandis - for fighting and destroying vampyres
A blessed silver sickle - blessed by the Nature Spirit, this will be very useful for refilling your druid pouch (see below).
A druid pouch - vital in fighting ghasts, you will need a filled druid pouch to make the ghasts visible and hence attackable. If your pouch is empty use a blessed silver sickle to cast 'Bloom' and harvest some nearby resources.


Ghasts are spread throughout Mort Myre and also inhabit the specialist routes you follow during the Temple Trek and Burgh de Rott Ramble activity. Ensure that you have completed the Nature Spirit quest in order to use the druid pouch.

While some items, such as a hammer and a knife, may be useful when trekking and rambling, you will be able to find these items in your area when you need them.

Making the Journey

The Villagers

The range of villagers you can escort to the Temple are as follows:

Rolayne Twickit

Rolayne is a lonely old man and quite hard to accompany, but he will help you get the greatest reward. Rolayne will try to defend himself but he is not a very capable fighter, so you will need to keep a close eye on him and stick together on the route to the temple.
This is where the food comes in handy. If Rolayne is taking heavy damage you can give him food to restore some of his health.		 Smiddi Ryak

Smiddi Ryak is also very hard to accompany, but can lead to the greatest reward. She will not be able to fight any attacking monsters and will try to stick with you.
Some food is necessary because she has a low Constitution level. It will be best to have a plan before trekking.
Jayene Kliyn

Jayene is a medium-level villager to accompany and will help you get one of the medium rewards. She is reasonably tough and can take a few hits. She can also try to defend herself and fight off other monsters within the event area.
Although she will not be a problem on the easy route, you may want to consider some food just in case she takes heavy damage.		 Valantay Eppel

Valantay is a medium-level villager and will help you get one of the medium rewards. He is quite tough and can damage some monsters. He can also try to defend himself and fight off other monsters within the event area.
For the hard route it will be wise to carry some food and some heavy armour to defend yourself and Valantay.
Dalcian Fang

Dalcian is an easier villager to accompany but will only offer you the chance to get low rewards. Dalcian is tough and can potentially fight other monsters. He will offer help in fighting in events and will try to defend himself as much as possible.
With the hard route however, it will still be tough against some of the bigger monsters like the snails and snakes. 		Fyiona Fray

Fyiona Fray is an easier villager to accompany but she will only offer you the chance to get lower rewards. She is tough and can potentially fight other monsters. She will offer help when fighting in events and will try to defend herself as much as possible.
With the hard route however, it will still be tough against some of the bigger monsters like the snails and snakes

The Adventurers

The range of adventurers you can escort to Burgh de Rott are as follows:

Forester

Foresters are not noted as great heroes in the annals of history, so it's little surprise that those rushing to Burgh de Rott are less than qualified.
This is where the food comes in handy. If the Forester is taking heavy damage you can give him food to restore some of his health.		 Woman-at-arms

The Woman-at-arms is a warrior in training, and in a few years she should be able to make her own solo expeditions, or even lead other adventurers to Burgh de Rott. In the meantime, though, her enthusiasm greatly exceeds her skill.
Some food is necessary because she has a low Constitution level. It will be best to have a plan before trekking.
Apprentice

The Apprentice is not yet a great sorcerer, and needs your help to become one. The apprentice is not a difficult adventurer to accompany, but is by no means simple.
Although he will not be a problem on the easy route, you may want to consider some food just in case he takes heavy damage.		 Ranger

The Ranger is a hardened warrior with quite a bit of experience, but the appeal of heroism is still greater than her skills are ready for...
For the hard route it will be wise to carry some food and some heavy armour to defend yourself and the Ranger.
Adventurer

The Adventurer is fairly easy to accompany but will only offer you the chance to get low rewards. He is tough and can potentially fight other monsters. He will offer help fighting in events and will try to defend himself as much as possible.
With the hard route however, it will still be tough against some of the bigger monsters like vampyres and nail beasts. 		Mage

The Mage is a powerful spell-caster intent on bringing salvation to the people of Burgh de Rott. She could probably do the ramble herself, though, so don't expect a significant reward.
With the hard route however, it will still be tough against some of the bigger monsters like vampyres and nail beasts.

The Routes to Paterdomus / Burgh de Rott

Path choices
Once you have decided on the villager you are going to guide, you will be taken to a screen where you can choose the route you wish to take to the Temple. During the trek to the Temple, you may experience a selection of events. These events should prove exciting and rewarding. You will have a choice of three routes.

Route 1: Easy This is the slowest route, but it poses the least danger as it zig-zags through Mort Myre and allows plenty of chances to evade events if they happen. If you experience an event along this route, you can attempt to evade it by reaching the "evade event point" and still go on to complete the trek and get a reward. This route is designed for low-to-mid level players with a reasonable Combat level.
Route 2: Medium This route is quite dangerous. The route follows the edge of the river Salve on the way up to Paterdomus. There are fewer opportunities for you to evade events. In these events the river is always on the left and reflects the lack of potential escape directions.

You need to defeat 50% of the enemies (if 5 total enemies then kill 3, if 3 total enemies then kill 2) in this area before you can evade an event. The route is designed for mid-level players with a fairly high Combat level.
Route 3: Hard This route is very dangerous. This is much faster and the monsters are aware of this. It would be foolish to take this route with a weak adventurer. Enemies will set up ambushes and it's impossible to evade them. You must defeat all of the monsters in the area and complete the event to continue your trek, otherwise, your only option is to escape.

The only way out is to finish the trek or to "Escape", which entails no rewards for your hard work. This route is designed for mid-to-high level players who fancy a challenge.


The following are your menu-optionss in the event. You will find these by right-clicking the leaves on the ground at certain places on the edge of an event area.
  • The Continue menu option can only be used once you have completed the given event. This could be solving a puzzle or killing all enemies.

  • The Escape menu option can be used to escape an event at any point. Escaping will forfeit any rewards and you will appear in the relevant area of Morytania.

  • The Evade menu option can be used to evade from an event and continue with the trek.*

*This is possible at any point on the Easy route, but it will increase your chances of getting an event again. In order to evade from the Medium route you will need to have completed 50% of the event. You cannot Evade from the harder route and must complete the event or use the "Escape" option.

Note: You cannot evade during an Obstacle Event on any route.

When you have selected your route, you will see the player icons move around on your route. At any point you may see '!' appear. Prepare yourself as this means you are about to enter an event. There are three types of event: 'Aggressive', 'Obstacle' and 'Gift giver'. You will need to complete the event to continue trekking or rambling and get to your reward.

Aggressive Events

Ghasts

Ghasts are well known inhabitants of Mort Myre. To complete this event you will need to kill all ghasts in the given area. To do this, use a filled druid pouch with the ghast to make it visible, then kill it. If you have a blessed silver sickle, but an empty druid pouch, locate some logs and cast 'Bloom' to fill your pouch. If you are unable to fight the ghasts, you will need to escape the event.


Shades

You will only be attacked by shades if you get an event in Mort'ton. Shades can be very tough and hit hard if not prepared. They will try to swarm your companion, so you must try to take the focus off of them if they are to survive.

Gadderanks, former tithe-collector of Morytania (defeated during In Aid of the Myreque), feared shades and had a special weapon constructed to give him an advantage against these beasts.


Vampyre Juvinates

As you have learnt in In Aid of the Myreque, you need a silver weapon to fight vampyres. To kill or even hit vampyres, you should use the Rod of Ivandis. Attack with this, or a sickle until the vampyre is at half health. When you are at this stage, use the staff's special attack to hold the vampyre. While the vampyre is paralysed, quickly use some Guthix Balance on him. Doing so will either kill him, cure him or make him angry and stronger!


Giant Snake

Giant snakes should not be underestimated. If they are allowed to attack together, they can inflict massive damage. It may be advised to evade them; however, you may find your reward greater if you take them on. If they are killed, a knife may be used to obtain snakehide, which can be used to craft snakeskin armour.


Giant Snail

Bigger by far than your average Myre snail, these snails are powerful Ranged-attack users. You must work hard and quickly if you are to defend your adventurer in this event. Note, though, that the shells of other snails provide surprisingly effective protection. As with the giant snakes, you may find your reward greater for overcoming the challenge of the giant snails.


Nail Beasts

Nail beasts are massive and belligerent creatures with vicious claws and teeth, which they use in a frenzy of biting and slashing. They'll wait in a defensive crouch before unleashing their horrific assault. It's best to attack them from a distance, then collect up the nails they drop, which can be used in a special potion.


Vyrewatch

If you have completed Legacy of Seergaze, the Vyrewatch will once more stalk the swamps of Mort Myre, hoping to discourage any and all from fighting back against the might of House Drakan. You can only hurt them with the Ivandis Flail, which makes it an essential piece of equipment if you intend to hazard the journey. Vyrewatch bodies can be collected and burnt in the Paterdomus Columbarium in the same way as Vyrewatch slain in Meiyerditch.


A creature from the depths...
If travelling on the hard route, you may receive an event during the boat trip. Stories tell of a giant, tentacled monster which must be killed if any further trekking efforts are to be made. The stories say that the beast has mystical powers of regeneration, so it may be more than a match for your average fighter.

Obstacle Events

The Bog

Mort Myre is a marshland after all, so don't be surprised if you come across a bog. Although seemingly impossible to cross, you will find that with the tools of the myre it is possible. If you brought any bladed weapon or knife with you then use it on a nearby spiked bush.

You will get three spiny sticks from doing this. Use your stick with a section of the bog to see if the ground is firm enough to cross (you may lose a stick randomly in the bog). When you have tested the bog with your stick, cross onto the firm section and continue testing the next part until you make it all the way across.

River pass

You may come across a river pass which gives no clues of how to cross. However, with a little ingenuity and investigation, you may find tendrils of ideas forming, which allow you to swing your way to freedom.

The Birth of a Ghast

Ghasts are the embittered spirits of those that have starved to death in the fetid swamps of Mort Myre. Sometimes you will come across parties that are starving and close to death, and you will have the opportunity to save them from this terrible fate.

Start by looking for some food and something to cook it with, then feed the refugees before they turn into ghasts.

The Broken Bridge

A broken bridge can be a pain, but with a mind as sharp as an axe it's easy to see the woods for the trees and fix your way to freedom.

In some cases, the wood you need will come looking for you, so you'd best be ready to fell some lumberjacks. You might also get some special equipment.

Abidor Crank

Some events may not be dangerous or puzzling. You may randomly find that a good samaritan will be strolling the myre and offer to help you. He will not only heal you, but turn any rotten food (from ghast encounters) into stew!


Rewards

If you manage to make it all the way from Burgh De Rott to the Temple on the Salve, or vice versa, the character you have guided will present you with a reward scroll.

Please note: If you make it through Mort Myre without encountering an event, you will only receive a small amount of the reward when handing in the reward scroll. This reward maybe smaller than the amounts shown in the table below.

There are three scrolls: blue, yellow and red. The one you receive is based upon which character you took on which route, and how you handled any events you encountered. For Temple Trekking, head to the Burgh de Rott bank to cash in this scroll for your reward; Ramble rewards can be redeemed by Hiylik Myna, at the Burgh de Rott Ramble start point.

You can receive the following items; again the amount you receive is dependant on your character choice, route choice and event performance. If you face a particularly large number of encounters, you may even receive an exceptional reward.
