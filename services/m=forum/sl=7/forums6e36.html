Ok, here are some useful phrases:

this is - to jest {pronouced to yest}
thank you - dziekuje {impossible to pronouce good without being born in poland, but close to "chekooye"}
can I have... - poprosze {poproshe} used while shopping
Excuse me - przepraszam {psheprasham}
good day - dziendobry {close to chendobre}
hi - czesc {close to cheshch}
see you - do zobaczenia {do zobatchenya}
good - dobrze {dobshe}
bad - zle {shle}
I love you - kocham cie {koham tche}
how are you - jak sie masz {yak she mash}
beer - piwo {peevo}
tea - herbata {herbata}
You're very attractive - podobasz mi sie {podobash mee tshe}
I don't understand - nie rozumiem {nye rosoomyem}

The next few useful phrases.
Yes - Tak (Tack)
No - Nie (Nyee)
Nice to meet you. Milo mi. (mee-WOH mee)
Where is ... ? - Gdzie jest ... ? (kdyee YEST ...?)
Where? - Gdzie? (Gje?)
Bus stop - Przystanek autobusowy
Shop - Sklep
That's all, thanks. To wszystko, dziekuje. (to -VSHEES-tkoh, DYEE-koo-yeh)
What time is it? - Ktora jest godzina?
My name is ... - Nazywam sie ... (nah-ZYH-vam syee)
What's your name? - Jak sie nazywasz? (Yak syee nah-ZYH-vash?)
Where is the bus to ...? Skad odjezdza autobus do ...? (skond od-YEH-zdha AH-oo-TOH-boos doh ...?)
How much is a ticket? Ile kosztuje bilet? (ee-LEH kosh-TOO-yeh BEE-let ?)
Maybe- Moze (Moshe)
What? - Co? (cho)
How? - Ile? (Eleh?)
How much is it? Ile to kosztuje? (Eleh toh KOSH-too-yeh?)
Please - Prosze
I'm here on holiday. Jestem na wakacjach. (YES-tem nah vah-KHA-tzyaah)
This is my first visit. To jest moja pierwsza wizyta. (toh MOH-yah PYEER-vshah vee-ZEE-tah)
I'm lost. - Zgubilem sie. (sgoo-BEE-wem syee.)
I've already written pronunciation and I added a few new ones.
If you have any questions write.
Have a good holiday!
