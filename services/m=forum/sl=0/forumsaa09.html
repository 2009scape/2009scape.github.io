The Jagex Cup 2010



What is the Jagex Cup all about?

Earlier in the month, the Jagex Clan Cup 2010 was launched and this is the second time that we are running this prestigious competition. Split in to three different sections of Combat, Skilling and a Combination of the two, it's set to get the pulses racing and provide a fantastic spectacle. For more information, and to keep track of the competition, head over to the The Jagex Cup forum; alternatively, here is a quick rundown to whet the appetite:

The Combat Cup is the bloodiest of the three sections, held within free-to-play Clan Wars - it sets an even battlefield for each side to prove that they're number one. Now with the addition of Sub-Divisions, clans can test their mettle with similar opposition with regards to combat level, F2P/P2P status and whether they are pures or not.

The Skilling Cup tests players' knowledge of the best training locations and, with the introduction of Buddy Skilling, how skills work in tandem with one another. Four skills and only eight weeks; it's going to put a strain on everyone's tools.

The Combination Cup is for those who know how to master the combination of teamwork, resource management and each of their skills to the best of effect. Held within the 'greyerific' Stealing Creation world, there will be more clay worked over the coming weeks than at the Crafting guild.

Coming soon in the Clan Submissions Blog

Mod Timbo gives us an insight into how the Jagex Cup was created in a mini-blog posted in the upcoming Clan Submissions Blog. Here is a taster:

The dust had finally settled on the Clan Wars floor after The Titans won last year�s Combat Cup and shortly after, planning for this year�s Jagex Cup went underway. We had been given a lot of feedback during last year�s competition through various methods, the feedback threads that we created at the end, various player created threads and posts and, of course, the occasional rant. There was no doubt in my mind that the next competition would be bigger and better than the one I had only just finished. The only question was how could it be any bigger?

V a


Just like every other player, I started as a noob in Lumbridge, with my starter kit and all. In the few years after that I had seen most of the lands of Gielinor, with my occasional visit to Lumbridge (sometimes voluntarily, and sometimes� Well� you know). I trained my skills, slayed some monsters, and made a few good friends.

I set myself some goals, and started working on them. I achieved some of my goals, but then... something happened.

An update came, and I found myself with a problem to which I could not imagine a solution. One of the items I regularly used got an update, and seemed to work differently than it used to, therefore making one of my statistics impossible to train. I was desperate, and I decided to post on the Forums (something I hardly ever did). I made a nice and constructive thread with my views and solutions to this item change.

My thread was up for a while, and it accumulated a lot of responses. After I got over 300 posts on my thread the good news came, a friendly J-Mod had a look at my problem and passed it along to the Content Team, and a week after that I got a post from the Content Team. It was finally getting fixed, and so it did!

In the time I spent running my thread I also had a look around the Forums. It was a place I had never seen before, and I loved it right from the start. I have had quite some adventures in-game, but what I found on the Forums really interested me. Almost every topic I can think of is covered! Recent Updates, guides, help with quests, skills, and so on. While I was running my thread I started posting here and there, giving others advice on what I thought was best, posting my support when I saw a good idea, or just placing a comment on one of the funny threads that were placed in the Off-Topic or General sections.

Not only do the Forums have places to leave a post, they also have a lot of ongoing business you might not have expected to find there. Yes, I�m talking about a great community of players that you meet on the Forums! Nowadays I regularly meet someone I saw on the Forums in-game, and I always have a nice chat with them. Alternatively, you can join forces with others and form a clan; I�ve seen some really interesting clans, and they're not only made for killing monsters, nooo, some of them train skills, others play mini-games, PvP, etc! And it�s not only that, there are also a lot of events hosted by players and that is something you have to check out sometime, it�s a great way to have fun and make new friends!

I knew it right from the start, I had fallen in love. Ever since the day I found the Forums I�ve been posting on them with a big smile.

You should definitely have a look around the Forums, and I hope to see one of your posts someday!
