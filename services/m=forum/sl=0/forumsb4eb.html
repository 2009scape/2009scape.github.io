Scenario: Such and such a player (who apparently lurks this forum occasionally!) plays the newb. After some small chat, he asks if I can help him out with gold. I hesitate and personally feel skeptical. However, I shrug it off because regardless of whether they're telling the truth, I am generous, and I know what it is to need help, and "doing unto others" is a pretty good standard to live by. Sure you can't be handing out your earnings all of the time, but the occasional gift won't set anyone back dramatically.

So, I chose to give it. It's pocket change that is easy to replace in less than a single trip to Taverly Dungeon or what have you. I was even prepared to give a little more, but with so many quest points lacking (on his side,) how could I?

Low and behold, said player turns around, flashes a much more substantial amount of cash in the trade window, proceeds to laugh at me and call me a noob, and then harrass me in private messages while turning it off just before I can politely retaliate.

The point is this: Don't shove someone's generosity toward you back in their face. You can say "Haha, I actually a godsword" and soforth, but what are you proving? Is it more impressive to be a wealthy player on runescape, or a kind and respectable person? Think about it. There are far fewer of the latter, but the latter get much further.

After all, you might have fibbed about how poor you were, but maybe for rather cryptic and more humble reasons, I also understated my wealth? That being said, at least I earned it all legitimately.
