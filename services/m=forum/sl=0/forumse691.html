This is the dungeoneering do's and don'ts thread.

I will post a few things and I would hope other could do so too. This is a thread to help people through the new skill with their perfecting your solo-ing experience or team experience.

----Solo--------

1. Always make gatestone and cure me runes. Fast and efficient. Also saves money for anti pots.

2. Pick up all the herbs and put in your base. Never know if you need it later on and don't want to waste time farming... if you know you have the herb somewhere.

3. If you are rushing, only kill monsters in the room if there is a GD (guardian door), room before the boss, or a room that requires you to make the statue parts or move statues.

4. Save the fishing for the end if need be.


====Teaming=====
1. The best floors are 30+ for teams, but I would do 25+.

2. Pick a person to pick up keys. This person will take most of the damage so... GIVE THEM FOOD! (so many times I have been on teams and the person who "dies the most" is the key holder.

3. Make gatestone and cure all runes. Cure all for the group... Be a sharer of magic wealth

4. Don't steal all the food on the ground. Only take a few pieces of food each time something is dropped.

5. NEVER ASK A PERSON TO MAKE YOU A PROMETHIUM ANYTHING! (Well if you are on pro teams). You should already have them silly!

6. Don't skill in there. Save your fishing for later. (if you are doing pro teams, friends it's ok)

7. At the end if your large floor is out of food (which is rare) just do that air rune trick. Buy air runes, make them, sell back to smuggler. Then you buy either salve eels, blue crabs or cave morays.

8. Call out everything! DE (dead end) GD (Guardian Door) BD (boss door)

9. Take turn on mazes and the imbuing tile floors. Or at least feed the person that gets damaged.

10. Be nice. Help each other out. The least times you die is the more xp you get.

-----Tips--------

Rammernaut and Riftsplitter just mage them to death.

Barlak the Pummeller is easy w/ promethium spear and a bunch of gatestones and maybe a good 10-20 pieces of spare food in the room next to the door.

Shadow forger is by far the worst boss and in teams, he can be quite hard. Make cure potions and if there is a hi lvl herblore-ist then just have them make strong cure potions.

======

Always make a gatestone outside the door of the boss. Always clear that room as well.

Never go in alone as a team... wait for the others, unless you will die fast.

========

pound on spiders
swipe at mysterious shades
stab at brutes


When entering a room with mysterious shades, kill them first. Then magers and rangers.
If you get poisoned, wait til you are done with the fight to cure, or else you will cure twice. It's not a pot, doesnt last for a few minutes.


If you see warriors with brightly colored armour (especially orange) you want to either mage them or crush them with your spear.

=======
(information by blessed fool)
If someone has a shadow hood bound make them the keyrunner because they can take a lot less damage when running in and out of rooms. Especially when theres a Primal warrior or Sagg ranger in the room.

You don't get less xp if you kill a dinosaur.

----
(info by Deirdrel)
Shadow forger, stat restore potions are a huge help with him. (If you have full prayer from an altar, stat restore potions are actually more useful than prayer potions.)

Jake, what Deirdrel was pointing out was that you don't need a tinderbox with you in order to light those pillars for Night-gazer. You can light them anyway. That saves an inventory space.

I like your courteous do's and don'ts for the most part, but I disagree with "save your fishing". I have often found when I end up in a team of higher levels that my time is better spent (and my efforts more appreciated) getting food for the rest of the team. Teams that also do not allow time to "kit up" at the end with adequate food for the boss also annoy me. That is discourteous itself when we're trying to avoid that death penalty. Therefore, I never save my fishing now ... if I see a good fishing spot, I grab it. If this bothers the rest of the team, I generally don't go with them again. Good skilling can be more important to a successful floor than rushing through to the end.
