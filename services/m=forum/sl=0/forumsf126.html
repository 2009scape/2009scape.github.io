While training dungeoneering I have noticed that the vast majority of players use melee. I started using melee when I first trained the skill simply because my stats were higher. However, once I saw that everyone used melee in team dungeoneering I decided to switch to a ranger or mage to help out my team.

Sadly this did not turn out the way I hoped. I soon found out that using both mage and range is very hard in group dungeoneering and even harder solo. I then understood why everyone used melee; it is far better than the other 2 classes.

There are 2 problems I found with magic and range when compared to melee. First they are far less efficient in obtaining both armor and the ammunition necessary to fight. Secondly they are weaker in combat abilities when compared to melee.

~~Efficiency~~

One of the largest problems with both range and magic is that they must create their own ammunition to fight while melee doesn�t have to do anything like that. Melee starts out with all the items it needs while range and magic must spend time making arrows and runes which requires a lot of time and high level skills.

For magic you must create runes out of pure essence obtained through shops and uncommonly droped by specific monsters. When you start out the dungeon you get up to 125 bound runes but to participate in combat you must craft the rest yourself which can be a large problem as you need to kill a few monsters with your staff to get the money to buy essence. Once you craft the essence you can fight for a few minutes but will soon have to return to craft more.

So, basically mage requires these things:
Doing all this will add 4+ minutes to your time per dungeon compared to melee.
You will need 77 runecrafting to craft blood runes.
At least 3 inventory slots to hold runes but will likely use up 4-6 for most players.

Range has it far worse than magic when it comes to creating ammunition. You start out with 125 bound arrows which will last you a while but you will soon enough run out and be helpless. You then have 2 choices, use a very weak melee weapon or make more arrows.

If you chose to make more arrows you will have to go through 8 steps which can be very time consuming. You need to mine ore, smelt the ore, smith the bar into arrow heads, cut logs, fletch those logs into shafts, buy feathers, feather those shafts, then finally add the arrow heads. This takes up a lot of inventory slots as well.

There is also another problem with making these arrows, you just won�t find many of the same ore and the chances are most of that ore will be lower tier. This means you will be making multiple arrow types, most of which are very weak.

So, basically range requires these things:
Making arrows will add at least 6 minutes to your time per dungeon.

In the tests I did 65% of the armor resources I found were ore rocks for melee armor, 15% were the dinosaurs for range armor, and 20% were plants for mage armor. This means you will run into ore rocks far more often than either dinosaurs or plants giving melee an even larger advantage.

~~Combat Strength~~

Another thing which I have noticed is that range and magic simply aren�t as strong as melee. Range falls behind in both damage and defense when compared to melee and magic falls way behind in defense but has a decent offense which in my opinion is fine.

What I see most players doing is binding a weapon and a chest piece to their character. I will compare the stats of tier 10 (level 90) equipment of the 3 classes.

Melee gets: +122 accuracy, +139 damage, around +200 defense against melee and range.
Range gets: +168 accuracy, +37 damage, around +85 defense against everything.
Magic gets: +61 magic, around +50 defense against melee and magic.

Based on these stats I would say melee is much better than range and close to magic in offensive stats. However, both magic and range fall way behind when it comes to defense.

If you ignore the stats however, you will see a large imbalance in the amount of melee creatures there are in dungeoneering when compared to range and magic creatures. From the research I did 55.3% of the monsters used melee attacks, 21.5% used range attacks, and 23.2% used magic attacks! This is a HUGE problem which makes magic and range even worse.

Now, you may think that having so many melee monsters would be good for mage but it is actually bad. You see, many of those melee monsters do not follow the typical combat triangle when it comes to defense. Since they don�t have a negative magic defense magic is equally matched in power or weaker than both melee and range.

Melee has the largest advantage against creatures simply because their armor is so much stronger and their weapon is just as effective, if not more. Because they can basically tank 76.8% of the creatures in the dungeon and use prayer against mages they will not have a problem while mage and range will need to use prayer in far more situations just to stay alive.

~~Conclusion~~

Based on this information, melee is far better in both efficiency and combat abilities. This is highly discouraging and unfair to anyone who wishes to use magic or range.

I was honestly hoping that Jagex would fix the mess which is the combat triangle within dungeoneering. They did a great job balancing the weapon stats but the combat triangle is far from fixed. Jagex said they are looking to fix the combat triangle within the main game but if they can�t even balance it out in dungeoneering, which is like a game in its own; there is no hope of it being fixed in the outside game.

Below I have listed suggestions on ways for Jagex to fix these problems.

~~Suggestion 1 - Class Sets~~

First of all, there should be �class sets� which you can switch between with your dungeoneering ring. You would either have to choose melee, range, or magic. Basically these class sets are for bound equipment. When you bind a magic item it would only be applied to your magic class set, range would only go to your range class set, and melee would only go to your melee class set. So, you will not have any bound runes or arrows while you are using a melee class set.

When you enter the dungeon with one class set active you would get all of your bound items in that set. You cannot toggle your class set while in the dungeon, only before you enter. Anything you bind to a class set would not use up bound slots in the other class sets.

Since these class sets would make dungeons much harder on solo dungeoneerers, players who solo will have access to all the items in 2 class sets. This will allow them to effectively hybrid as certain bosses and situations require multiple combat styles.

Now, you�re probably wondering why this is necessary to fix the combat classes but it should become apparent later in my suggestions. This will also make it so players aren�t stuck using 1 combat class as you can only bind a total of 4 items at max which makes it much more effective to just stay with a single class.

~~Suggestion 2 - Changes to Consumable Ammunition~~

The current amount of runes and arrows you can bind is far too small to make range and magic efficient. You will have to constantly be making more which is a huge disadvantage to melee, not to mention that many of your inventory slots will be used up because of this.

The amount of arrows and runes you can bind should be increased as you level up. At level 1 you should be able to bind up to 150 arrows and 1,500 runes. Every 7 levels after that you will be able to bind 60 more arrows and 600 more runes (up to a max of 1170 arrows and 11,700 runes at 120 dungeoneering).
This amount of runes may seem high but keep in mind; some of the higher level spells use 10 runes each cast.

Next, get rid of all the different types of runes and replace them with 1 type, the new omni-rune. This rune will cover all rune types and you will be able to equip it to your "arrow" slot.

The amount of runes you can make via runecrafting will also be changed. At 1 runecrafting you can make 1 rune per essence, at 9 you can make 2 runes per essence and every 9 levels after that you can make 1 more rune per essence up to 12 per essence at 99 runecrafting. To make obtaining rune essence easier there should be "essense veins" which can be built in the starting room with construction.

Range should also get an "Ava�s accumulator" effect so they don't litter the ground with distracting arrows.

This will make these 2 classes much more efficient. Like melee, they will be able to complete a dungeon without having to worry about ammunition.

~~Suggestion 3 - Stregthering Range Weapons~~

Currently range is weaker in offensive abilities than both melee and range. It has a decent accuracy but the problem is its arrows have an extremely low damage bonus.

Its damage needs to be strengthened to compete with melee weapons. Many players have suggested that the arrows get a range bonus damage increase but I feel that would unbalance the shortbow and longbow. My suggestion is that the shortbow gets something like a +15% damage increase and the longbow gets a +30% damage increase much like staffs do.

~~Suggestion 4 - Reworking of Monster Types and Resources~~

The amount of melee monsters and ore is far too great when compared to the other types.

The amount of plants for mage armor, dinosaurs for range armor, mage monsters in the dungeon, and range monsters in the dungeon all need to be increased. Having such a high percentage of melee monsters and ore is not acceptable!
