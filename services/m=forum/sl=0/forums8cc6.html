Summarized Guide.
���������������

Here is a brief summary of the Simple Guide to Signature Making, the three posts following this one.

1: Open up a word document (Arial, Font 9) or a new post. You will use this to make your signature.

2: Choose your text. Can be Normal Text, ��t ��׆, L33t t3xt, or a mixture. Can also be Neat or MeSsY. It cannot be more than 4 lines long, and can contain no rule breaking statements. Letters not found on your keyboard can be found in post 6.

3: Choose your border from posts 4 and 5. Make sure it's something you like. Insert it into the new post or Word Document.

4: If a censor is affecting your signature, change around letters or symbols to fix it. If all else fails, put a (c) before each line of the signature.

5: Center the text of each line over the rest, and be sure the border is symmetrical.

Simple Guide to Signature Making- The Basics.
�������������������������������������

For those of you that want to keep this simple, this is the place for you. This guide was originally intended to make signature making simple, and it will continue to do that under my ownership. If you understand everything here and want to go on, procede to post 11.

~Choosing Your Text~
First things first, making the text in your signature.

There are several types of text. Normal text is regular text typed using only the keyboard letters. Alt text uses alt codes to make letters. If you don't know how to do alt codes, simply copy and paste them from the list in post 6. Not all letters have alt codes, so you'll have to use regular letters for them. Leet text uses numbers and symbols found on the keyboard to make letter-like shapes. Full leet is rarely used, as it is difficult to read.
This is normal text.

Text can also be messy or neat. Neat is perfect capitalization, while messy is mixed capitals and lowercase.

or something to that extent. Whatever you choose, be sure that it isn't vulgar or obscene. Also, it cannot be more than 4 lines long, like the rest of the signature.

~Choosing A Border~
Starting in post 4, there is a list of borders. Choose one of these that strikes your fancy. Paste it onto a new post, or a word document, or whatever works for you, along with your text. ~The Censor~
If you haven't noticed yet, then you probably will sometime soon. Those dreaded "stars" keep appearing in your signature! What are they doing there??? I didn't put those there! Well, that's the evil censor monster attacking your signature. The censor is the filter put in by JaGeX to prevent offensive language and other verbal rule breaking. In short, it can be a real pain.

~Fixing It~
So here are some things you can do:

Use different letters.
The letters are actually the most common problem. Replace the letters one at a time, clicking the "Preview Post" button near the "Add Message" or "Update Message" Button, after each replacement. Repeat until the signature doesn't star out. If you have already posted the signature, then you must repost the orignal version in its place, and then go on to fix it. Don't ask why.

Using a different Border
Sometimes, the border is what's causing the asteriks (yes, that's what they're really called ) to appear. Try using a different border, or replacing one of the symbols in the border with another one.

When all else fails...
Place a "(c)" just before your signature. This often works, but it doesn't look as good. If the problem isn't solved with just one (c), then put one before EVERY line in the signature, like this:
