Off Topic Debate Threads Sign Up



This Sign Up thread is now CLOSED


Greetings all,

I will soon be launching a trial run of some Official Off Topic debate threads as mentioned in the previous 'Have Your Say' thread. With luck these will be weekly threads which should stir up some interesting discussions.

How the debates will work


Before the start of the debate this Sign Up thread will be opened showing the new topic. You will then be able to sign up for the debate by stating whether you would be for or against. Once a suitable size of users has been reached the sign up will be closed and the debate opened.

The debates will be free form allowing anyone who signed up to take part whenever and as often as they like. The debates will be open for one week. Only those who signed up may take part and they will be listed on the start of the debate thread. Anyone not listed will have their post hidden and arguments ignored.

During the debate points will be awarded for good arguments and whichever side has the most points at the end of the week will be deemed to have won the debate. Points will be awarded thusly:
  • Average argument post = 0 points
  • Good argument post = 1 Point
  • Excellent argument post = 3 points
Points will also be deducted for rule breaking posts (And those responsible removed from the discussion).

How to make good arguments


At first I (Mod Crow) will be running the debates and acting as the overall judge. This means I will choose which arguments I deem to be good. Here are a few tips to making a good argument:
  • Use your own opinions
    Whilst quotes listing facts are perfectly valid I'm not interested in hearing what someone else personally feels about a topic. I want to hear your arguments and so I'll be happier to give points if you make a claim using your own reasoning than if you use the reasoning of another.
  • Read your opponents arguments
    The best debates occur when someone acknowledges what their opponent has said and then counters that argument. Turning someone's argument against them is always good for a discussion.
  • Be clear and to the point
    Try and keep your arguments short and sweet. Rambling on for many posts to make a point which could have been made in two lines will only slow down the debate.
  • Respect your opponent
    These are intended to be fun debates, not battles. If you are respectful towards your opponent whilst crushing their argument you'll be showing how skilled you really are.
  • Bad grammar is not the end of the world
    Few things make you look pettier than if you focus on someone else's spelling and grammar rather than their argument. Remember that English may not be everyone's first language so you're not going to win any points by trying to attack their spelling.


The future


Currently these debates are in a trial period and will be completely decided by myself or another J Mod. Should they take off and remain fairly free of issue then we'll look at opening them up to the Off Topic community, including OT judges and topics you can submit yourselves.

Debate Rules


  • All Forum Rules Apply
    This means that the debate threads should not involve political/religious/inappropriate discussion, URL's, flaming, spam and so on.

  • No Flaming
    Whilst mentioned in the previous rule this is an important rule to remember when dealing in debates. These are intended to be fun threads and so you should only ever attack the statement rather than the person who made it. There is never a need to resort to any form of insult.

  • Stay on topic
    You're here to present an argument on a given topic, as such please ensure you keep to the topic and do not deviate.

  • Only post if you are signed up
    If you are not listed at the start of the debate then please do not post. Your arguments will be hidden and action may be taken against repeat offenders.

  • Never take it personally
    Debates can be quite involving and it can therefore be upsetting if someone 'defeats' you. Please remember that these debates are just for fun. If you find yourself getting worked up about the topic then there is no shame or harm in taking a break or leaving the discussion if it is really getting to you.

  • Do not challenge the result
    The end result will be based on points earned from good arguments not from any particular belief the judge may hold. We therefore ask that you not challenge the end result of the thread as that in itself can lead to flaming and disruption.
