.::�|�::. RSB Ladder Officials Forums .::�|�::.

This forum was created because after repeated observations by Aurorashines and Ms Kornfreak it was found that the quality of reffing has been somewhat laxed and no real training has ever been set up for new people wanting to become refs. After careful thought and planning, they then decided that it would be a great ideal to set up a sort of training camp for refs.

It expanded into a formal training system, so that new refs can receive the proper training they require and provide a more consistent service to the RSB community. So the result is this new ref forum where we have set up a mentoring program for new refs. Experienced refs will take new refs under their wings and show them how to do their duties in a manner that is the same as the next person. That way there is no conflict between decisions made one day versus the next just because there is a different ref.

Being a ref is an honor and a responsibility. Those who accept this position are saying the job will be done right.

Below are details that will help you to understand the different jobs and duties. If you just want to ref then fine but if you want to be a driving force for helping to constantly improve the ladders then look into becoming a mentor to help someone else. Mentors are chosen by their skill and activeness not by their popularity.

Good luck everyone and remember to always be consistent and fair in your decisions.

.::�|�::. Guidelines for the RSB Refs .::�|�::.
���������������������������������

The job of a ref is something that is very valuable to the RSB Community. These individuals help to maintain a fair and prosperous community. No one is questioning the honor of the clans of the RSB - we only wish to help to make sure that the rules that not everyone is familiar with are followed. In many cases clans are on multiple ladders and even in those ladders may be on more than one League/Division. This can lead to confusion and accidental violations of various rules.

Duties of a ref:

1. Verify that the declaration has been properly posted by the declaring clan.
First start a declaration thread then post as follows using the template provided on the first page of the Ladder Thread:
Declaration thread
Their clan thread
Their opponents clan thread
The Ladder thread

2. Make sure they have set and all have agreed on the date, time and various options for the war.

3. Make sure that both clans have agreed and posted their ref choices.

4. Make sure both clans have added both refs to their cc for access during the war and that they understand the following:

A. No killing the ref
If a ref is killed on one team but not the other and it is due to a blatant act and not accidental and the other ref is not killed in that round then the team that killed the ref will forfeit the one round of the war.

B. No kicking a ref from the cc
If you the ref is kicked then the clan must either reset the cc or set up a new cc for the war. If the ref is not allowed back into the cc before the war begins the clan will forfeit each round of the war that the ref is not able to enter the cc.

5. Verify both clans members lists were posted in the proper time line of 24 hours prior to the war unless otherwise agreed. Note no edits of a members list are allowed if a change must be made they are required to re post the entire list as a new post. This has to be prior to the 24 hour time line

Take pictures of any flaming that takes place and or violations of the gear requirements for the leagues that have gear restrictions.
Please remember clan spamming gl so and so or so and so ftw is allowed.
Flaming is defined as being mean words another person and trying to hurt their feelings or bad language.

8. After the war the ref is to verify the member�s lists with the people in attendance to the war.
If anyone shows to have been at the war not listed then this should be brought to the attention of the RSB thread holders who will then based on the number and the type of violation will rule on the issue.

9. Monitor clans for flaming. Remember friendly banter is one thing and is not a flame. If they become to insulting or taunting then that is when flaming is an issue. Take snapshots of any flaming and if in doubt ask a mentor ref or one of the senior/Lead/council members.

.::�|�::. Ref Ranks and Duties .::�|�::.
����������������������������

Senior Mentor:
Job: To help organize and support the refs.
If you have any problems first go to a Lead mentors and if not possible then speak to your Senior representative and they will be happy to assist you.

Lead Mentor:
They are here to help you through the learning process of becoming a ref. In order to be considered a full ref you must first be approved by your Lead ref and your a Senior Mentor before you are allowed to ref without observation.
If you have a problem with your Lead Mentor then please contact a Senior Mentor.

Mentor:
These are individuals who have show activeness as refs, ability to do their jobs well, and are good at teaching other people to meet the standards of the Ladders.
They are here to help and teach you so it is important that you take advantage of their knowledge and experience to become a great addition to the refs of the RSB.

Ref:
A person that has completed their training and is now a full ref and does not require a mentor to oversee them in their duties.

Trainee:
A person accepted who has applied as a ref but has as yet not completed their training process.


To become a mentor a ref must:

1. Be Active
2. Be Knowledgeable and a Respected Ref
3. Be Sponsored by a Lead or Senior mentor
4. Be Able to Teach Others Without Putting Them Down or Degrading Them

Community Representative:

Vote on issues and be the voice of the community.

To become a community rep you must:

1. Be Active
2. Be a mentor or higher
3. Be supportive and not flame others
4. Be voted in by the other Committee representatives
5. May not be a council seat on any of the three main ladders

Definition of Honor:

1: Good name or public esteem: REPUTATION
2: One whose worth brings respect or fame
3: A keen sense of ethical conduct: INTEGRITY


Examples of No Honor:

1. Mass Recruitment

-Defined by recruiting in Massive amounts.
-Example:
-Setting up a location at Pest Control or GE and spamming �Join The Great Valor 108+!�

Example: TGV declare on TRS. TGV then begin mass recruiting to go from 40 members to 80 and take on TRS who only recruited a normal 5!


2. Excessive Flaming

-Please remember clan spamming gl so and so or so and so ftw is allowed.
-Flaming is defined as being mean words another person and trying to hurt their feelings or bad language.


3. Unlisted/Multiclan Members

-Unlisted:
-Bringing individuals to a war that are NOT a true member of your clan and their only purpose is to fight in a war.

Multiclan
Bringing individuals who belong to not just your clan, but another clan as well.

Example: Turkeyboy is in TGV, TRS, Spirit PC List3: Being a member of 2 clans is NOT allowed, but a member of a Minigroup such as Pest Control is allowed.


4. Crashing another Clan�s War

-Simple as crashing another clan's Planned fight.
-Example: TGV vs TRS 20v20 matched PvP, FDR crash while they are in the middle of the war.

.::�|�::. Universal Ladder Rules .::�|�::.
������������������������������


These rules apply to all leagues however additional rules are in place for each league.

1. All Jagex rules apply to the Thread, and the Wars organized on it.

2.(Exception to this rule DM League)

No Multi-Claning.
Multi-Claning will not be tolerated in this Ladder.
(Bringing unlisted to war is the same as multi-clanners)

3. No Flaming. Flaming of any sort will be dealt with using the Grade system
(see further into the thread).

4. For rules related on who you can declare on see the individual ladder for more information.

5. You have five days to accept or decline a War, after being informed.
Once accepted you have 3 days to complete final negotiations and set date of war.
Date of war cannot exceed 14 days from the date the war is confirmed. This gives you a
Maximum of 22 days from day war is declared. 5 for declaration 3 for negotiations and 14 for
The war to take place.

6. You may not declare on a Clan under Immunity
Temporary immunity starts when a declaration is made for both the declaring and the declared upon clan.
Immunity for wars are only good for 5 days or until the war is accepted and ends when the war
Is either fought or the 22 day maximum time has expired.

7. You may not declare, if you are in Anti-Immunity.

8. (Does not apply to DM League)

You must post an accurate Member list 24 Hours from the War.

Clan will be issued 5 days anti immunity if posted less than 24 hour but more than 12 hours prior to war.
If less than 12 hours before war and opposing team requests the war will be awarded to the team that complied with the rules.
(Note for punishment or review of these rules are at the discresion of the ladder thread holder)

9. All cheating in a War should be posted with evidence on the results thread.

10. When defending, the defending clan cannot cross the middle line/barrier or the arena before the attacking clan engages in attacking.
