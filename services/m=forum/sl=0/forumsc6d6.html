"With the resources available on the Internet, there is no longer a need for teachers and schools"

I am for this statement. Teachers are merely needed for the understanding of a subject at schools, rather than the actual teaching of the subject. So yes, in a way, you could argue that without teachers teaching children at school, the children at a school would not understand. However, you can only truly understand a subject when you have interest of it, and therefore if you are not interested in the subject the teacher cannot help you understand it, and hence the teacher is not needed.

On the other hand, the internet has influenced the lives of most children in the world, since they have been introduced to it at a very early age. Due to this, they will have engaged an early interest in computers and the internet, and so they would find it more enjoyable if they learned their subject from resources online. The enjoyment of a subject causes the increase in understanding, and therefore in effect it eliminates the need for a teacher.
