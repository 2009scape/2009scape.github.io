Chrishankhah

Simple:

Bots may be more stupid than players in some respects, but some bot programs are actually a lot more proficient than human players. It's like the difference of typing something in an online dictionary and asking a human to look it up in physical book by hand. Of course the computer is going to get it faster, provided the computer

So take Puro Puro, for instance (the big corn maze with imps flying all about.) A bot is able to read the program and plot out the quickest path to the imp, something that a human mind can't altogether calculate at once in the same way.

A player's only hope is to patiently spend enough time there that they can, a) detect the patterns of a bot, and b) learn to outsmart the bot. A good skill to gain, but why should we have to do all this when it wasn't even part of the game's initial programming or intention?

We don't need to do a lot of thinking to see that bots aren't just. They are an attempt to bend the rules and manipulate the system for personal gain at others' cost -- the real world equivalent of which is criminal and punishable by law. They demonstrate greed and sloth on the part of the user and they cheat legitimate players out of the same opportunities because they are made to be more proficient than most ordinary players. Areas of the game which were previously slightly competitive suddenly become ridiculously nerve wracking and impossible to win at -- whether they be spots in a mini-game, hunter catches, kills, or what have you.

Oh -- Support, for Tizona.

Though I'm skeptical that the bot took from your inventory? That would be a serious breach if it could.

I also hope that we're sure all of what we accuse to be a bot actually is. Botting is a serious problem, but false accusations are another problem I've observed on RS.
