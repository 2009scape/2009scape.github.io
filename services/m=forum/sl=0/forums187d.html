Tensions between Misthalin and Asgarnia have tightened. A centuries long treaty has expired. The hope of keeping the peace that has existed for hundreds of years is gone. War is inevitable.

In an act of desperation the King of Varrock sends a messenger to the capital of Asgarnia; Falador, for the sake of recreating a treaty. Falador, believing that King Roald was in support of the assassination against their King, refused this treaty. The hand of the messenger was sent back to Misthalin along with a declaration of war.

Will you help defend Misthalin or lead the attack with Asgarnia? Or would you rather sit on the sidelines watching?

__July_11th__________________________________________________________________

-Arllani recieves a mysterious letter from the late King Roald III. Not knowing what to make of it, he goes to Varrock palace with Jon the Mercenary, where a mysterious masked man is waiting for him. After conversing briefly, the man seems to teleport Arllani away.

-Jon, using his years of experience, tracks the man to a palace atop a forzen hill. The man seems to be a powerful magician, and traps both Jon and Arllani in a room and begins to interrogate them. After only a bit of unfruitfull interrogation, both Jon and Arllani begin to attack the magician, forcing him to use strong spells that collapse the magic barrier that was barring them in.

-Arllani runs out, leaving Jon trapped with the magician, but shortly returns with arms to fight the man again. the battle leads them out of the room, where a Toxitis and Alexandria Vega confront the magician, who teleports away. The adventurers converse, then depart there seperate ways.

Name: Alexandria Vega
Age: 18
Gender: Female
Race: Human
Characteristics: bites her lip alot, usually found in robes, armour only when necessary
Generic personality: Shy around lots of people, but totally loyal for any cause she believes in.
Affiliation: Whoever can convince her they're right.
Occupation: Fisherman, lumberjack, miner, chef, warrior
Background: Born into a community cesspool, parents were not known there. Everyone was a parent. Everyone was a sibling. The community raised you. You were taught multiple skills, and then continued to train your best skills. Most children from that area had 2 skills and a mastery in a fighting style. Between the fishermen, lumberjacks, incinerators, miners, blacksmiths, Magic supplymen, crafters, and cooks, many balanced their skills out with one that fed the other. Many blacksmiths had mining so they could gather their materials straight and not waste money buying materials. Most cooks also fished as they could catch and cook, rather than buying from others. Magic supplymen were excellent miners to gather the essence for the runes they supplied to master magicians. Magicians were of rare quality, most never having the patience for firing spells from distances and running away. Archers seemed to have problems being found as many children developed problems with sight in the poor quality of living they called home. Almost everyone was a straight warrior, only knowing how to wield a sword or bludgeoning weapon. Alex trained in everything she could. She felt the need to be useful. Someone needed cover from long distances, she was decent with a bow. Someone was struggling against a heavily armoured foe, she would grab runes and provide distractions and support. She even excelled at clearing dungeons of their taint. Her mastery, however, still showed through in her warrior traits and high skills as a warrior and her other skills.
