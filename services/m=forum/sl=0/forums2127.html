29-Jun-2010 11:57:41
Remember back in the day, every time you even thought about certain skills... You'd see a wave of level 3s with the same exact clothes and lack of hair? I'm afraid this may come again.

About a month ago, I got 99 woodcutting by cutting ivy. I did it the legit way, by clicking an ivy every time my laptop stopped making the *chop, chop, chop* noise while reading a Stephen King novel. During this time, I came to recognize when people were clearly botting. The easiest way was when there were multiple bots, because they'd always move in sync. They'd always move one spot right. If there was no ivy one spot right, they'd move one spot left. If none there, they'd move two spots right. They followed that pattern, and they were always in sync. Another way I got to tell quickly, they will ignore *dozens* of tele-others without a word. Obviously, a real player would get mad and turn off accept aid at some point. The botters would just keep declining, and back to chopping. I'd report them, come back the next day to find the same bots there.

I rarely ran into more than three bots a day getting 99 woodcutting. Today, I got bored.. Took a stroll into a non-member world. I found 21 botters. Two groupings of willows south-west of Port Sarim, and the grouping of Yews in Rimmington. I understand that these could just be players (who like to move in sync). However, after harassing, calling them bots, telling them I'm going to report them, they did not reply or so much as notice me.

So, here's my concern, Jagex. Please work on more anti-botting measures! Encourage your player mods to look for them, add some random events, put something that detects bots in the game code. I earned this woodcutting cape and I have no respect for it because it seems like no one ever actually earns woodcutting with all these bots around.

Thank you for your time,
All Memory
