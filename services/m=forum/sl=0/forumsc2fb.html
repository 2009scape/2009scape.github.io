Welcome everyone to the Adopt a Writer Program!

This thread is designed to help out some of the newer, less experienced writers that visit these boards by pairing them up with one of the story forum's veterans. The available mentors will take those young padawans interested under their wings and help them grow and develop in their writing. These mentors would be a great resource by helping introduce their padawan to the story forum, showing them the ropes and how things work around here, answering any questions, and just being a source for feedback and criticism.

Now you might be wondering why you should get involved with this program?

Besides karma and the warm fuzzy feeling you get from helping others, by mentoring someone you're making it much more likely for that person to keep returning to the story forum and drawing them in as another friend and member of the community, thereby making this place more active which benefits everybody. And for padawans you have the opportunity to not only improve your writing, but get to meet and make friends with a lot of interesting people and get involved in this wonderful little community we have here.

Interested? Then keep on reading.

Potential Mentors:


When in comes to those interested in becoming a mentor, I am looking for 4 basic things:

1. You have to be a talented and knowledgeable author.

2. You have to have experience and know how things work around here. If the person you're mentoring asks a question like how do they get more readers on their story or where do they go for bios, and you don't know how to answer, then you're not helping them.

3. You have to be active. You're of no help if you're never online.

4. No jerks allowed. A mentor needs to be someone who's friendly and treats other people with respect. I don't care if you're the best writer on these boards. If you're just going to flame or insult your adopted writer whenever they ask you a question, then you're not welcome.



Mentor Application: (If I'm familiar with you and I think you would be a good candidate then I may allow you to bypass the application, although I would still like the name and quickfind code to whichever story you think best demonstrates your writing and maybe a little description about you to help with the pairings)
