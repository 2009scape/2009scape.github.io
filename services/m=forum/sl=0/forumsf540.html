History of the Mahjarrat
������������������
The Mahjarrat were brought to Gielinor from the world of Freneskae. Icthlarin, the desert god of the dead, took the Mahjarrat with him when he arrived in Gielinor. They took an 'Oath of Obedience' before their arrival on Gielinor, which means Icthlarin did*�t enslave them but hired them like soldiers. The Mahjarrat never saw Ichtlarin as a leader. According to Queen Senliten, the Mahjarrat fought against Zaros when he first started to invade the desert from the North. For years the Mahjarrat served Icthlarin but they left him and started following zaros, the Mahjarrat soon became Zaros' greatest soldiers.

Two different theories exist about their shift in leadership.
One suggests that Icthlarin abandoned them for their violent behaviour and the fact that their presence "warped" his sister, Amuscut and turned his full attention to aiding his father Tumeken.
Another says that Zaros offered the Mahjarrat power in his growing empire, and that the Mahjarrat gladly accepted the offer.

Zaros conquered the other gods and created an unrivalled empire in Gielinor. Nearly all of modern-day Misthalin, Asgarnia, northern Morytania, and the Wilderness soon came under his rule. The Mahjarrat, his greatest soldiers, reveled in the bloodshed.

The Zarosian Kingdom was one of great prosperity, but the *ahjarrat were not content. They became divided and began to work towards their own goals. The most significant of these was Zamorak(yes the one that now is a god). Zamorak was amongst the strongest beings to ever walk Gielinor. He earned himself the nickname "The Scourge of the Battlefield" proving his power in combat times. This earned him favour with Zaros, who quickly gave him the rank of general. Yet, despite this, Zamorak was not willing to serve Zaros, and even as his kingdom expanded from Senntisten(from edgeville to the digsite), Zamorak plotted against him.

The Staff of Armadyl(a powerfull artifact) eventually fell into Zamorak's hands. With the assistance of individuals such as Zemouregal, Lucien, Lord Drakan, Hazeel, Viggora, and Enakhra, Zamorak devised a way to defeat the Zaros.

A great battle raged in Zaros' greatest stronghold. Zamorak attacked Zaros somewhere in the castle. While Zamorak fought, his allies ensured that none of Zaros' bodyguards would interfere. Zamorak impaled Zaros with the Staff. However, Zaros was still immortal and started hitting Zamorak, when than Zamorak also got impaled by the staff. The staff impaled both Zaros and Zamorak, and, acting like a lightning rod, transferred Zaros' power into Zamorak. Zaros faded from existence on Gielinor's plane, while Zamorak seemed to become even more real. Now a god, and infused with enough power to rival the other deities of Gielinor, Zamorak went on to conquer Zaros� empire. Not all Mahjarrat wanted to betray Zaros and some tried to stop this event from happening.

The gods all agreed that Zamorak must be punished. Even as Zamorak stood triumphant, the gods banished him from Gielinor. Not long after his banishment Zamorak returned his godly powers now fully developed. Calling upon his former warriors, Zamorak declared war upon all that opposed him and began the God Wars. Throughout the Wars, the Mahjarrat would prove a good soldiers on the battlefield. The Zamorakian Mahjarrat waged war upon what few Zarosians remained. Most were forced into the Kharidian Desert, where they live as bandits today. The Zarosian Mahjarrat were all imprisoned, killed, or forced into hiding. Four thousand years into the Wars, Guthix awoke from his sleep. Seeing the horrors that had been unleashed upon his world, he demanded an end to the Wars. Guthix banished all the Gods from Gielinor, including Zamorak, though some Mahjarrat continue to serve him even today. By the end of the wars, only a handful of Mahjarrat still survived. Though they continued to serve Zamorak and Zaros, they became very divided in their actions. Today, the Mahjarrat are still working towards an unknown goal, and a few can still be found in the far reaches of Gielinor. While the Zamorakian Mahjarrat continue to increase their power and expand their conquered territory, the few Zarosian faithful remain in hiding and secretly prepare for the revival of Zaros. Most are now travelling to The North to complete The Ritual in order to rejuvenate their lost power while sacrificing one of their number.
