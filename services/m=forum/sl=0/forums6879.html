It depends on what you mean by tactics (what to do once inside the arena, or what to bring and how to fight) but I play FOG quite a lot (I have made 2 mil in F2P from it) and I have some tactics (these tactics are for F2P).

What to bring: For armor I bring rcing robes and green d'hide. Most people mage in FOG, so I use the green d'hide when I am being hunted. I find that green d'hide is enough to tank out most people, even if they melee.

I use the rcing robes for the hunter role, this gives me the abilty to hybrid (very important if you want to win) and also have high enough mage bonus to bind my target if they are a runner.

Dont bring full rune if you can help it, it is heavy and not very effective, since people are provided with runes, you are just asking to be maged.

For weapons I primarily use range (so, a maple shorbow) and have a fast melee wep for backup (I bring a rune scimmy)

I dont normally bring a staff, wielding melee or range weps gives you no negative mage bonus, and makes it easier to quickly switch attack styles, eavading your opponents prayers.

Do not bring a shield, it has no use as the stone that collects charges is 2 handed, and why would you need a shield to attack with?

As for tactics inside the arena, I have a few favourites.


1) delayed running: grab a stone and run to the wall. I try to wait there until I have between 15 and 21 charges. The aim of this is for your opponent to get to the center, see your not there and run to look for you. You aim to tele after he has run away from the center, so that you have some time in the center not being attacked while he is looking around for you

2) Running Rocks: Use the rocks near the center, run around them, aiming to trap your opponent on one side of the rocks and you on the other, interupting his melee attacks. The aim of this is to try to force your opponent to only use one or two attack styles (mage or range, as they can hit over the rocks, while melee gets trapped) which makes switching prayers alot easier on you, meaning you take less damage.

3) Hiding: This only works on a busy FOG world. Go to to center as fast as possible and hide yourself in the crowd. when you see your opponent use the minimap to run around in the crowd, making it so much harder for your hunter to find you. once he hits you, dont stop running. Pray whatever style your hunter is attacking with and keep running from place to place (making sure to stay hidden in the crowd, where your hunter will have to search through lots of names to find yours) this will force your hunter to find you every time he wants to switch attack types, this means that you dont have to switch protect prayers all the time, meaning more of the hits you take have reduced damage.





Using the portals is not effective (as well as being very anoying) you always get far more charges at the center, if your opponent is a higher level than you or you are taking too much damage too quickly, just use method #2, and you will survive for far longer.



Also remember to always pray, in both roles, your prayer gets recharged at the end of each role. Also, steel skin helps so much in the hunted position when used alongside a protect pray

Being an avid FOG player, I've seen many tactics, some fail and others be good. I'll make a list of hunter/hunted tactics.
Hunter:
-EQUIPMENT
~Necessary
Leather/dragonhide, Mage robes, a fast melee weapon, enchanted amulet, (depends on your combat style), Defenders (members only)
~Optional
Melee armor, bow, staff
~Don't bring
Shields. Dead wieght, unless it's a berserker shield, and even then it's hardly worth it.

-SEARCHING
~Easy opponents
When you start as a hunter, run straight to the center and wait about 5-10 seconds. If you opponent doesn't show up, run to where you opponent is. Chances are, he was there waiting for you, and teles away. Now the fight's usually easy, since they don't run from the center
~Medium opponents
Same deal as before, except when you get to their spot and they aren't there, you need to start searching. The best way I've found out how to search is to go to the center, go one direction, say north, make a big loop in a quarter of the arena so you can see the wall, and you should enter the center from the west or east to recharge your run energy. By the 4th time you'll have found your runner, he'll have teleported, and you'll have an easy fight.
~Annoying opponents
These people will probably be using the buildings after you've spent a good amount of time finding them. The best way to scout them out is to run to a building, wait a few seconds, run to another, wait a few seconds, etc, until you find them. Since they can't go into a building right away, bind them (if you can) and start hacking away. Repeat this process whenever they enter a building.
-FIGHTING
The only trouble you'll have is with prayer swappers/armor swappers. Just change your combat style fast enough to confuse them.

Hunted:
-RUNNING
~General
Keep your dragonhide on while you're running. Many people use mage so this is the best bet for a surprise attack.
~Wall Camping
This tactic includes finding a nice spot along the wall, and sitting for 5 minutes while your hunter fnids you, then teleing when he does. This isn't effective, you'll only get about 100-200 charges before they find you.
~Sweet Spot
This tactic is finding a spot near enough to the center to gain good charges while being far enough to not be seen. This is usually done in a building with an indent, so you can see your opponent coming. When they find you, run or tele to the center
~Reach Around
This tactic is one I personally use and laugh at how great it works. Bascially, instead of running straight to the center, you run completely around the arena while your hunter is running through the center, then when you are at the spot where your hunter started, run into the center. Your hunter is at your spot, running around the wall looking for you, while you're in the center, gaining 28 charges per charge. By the time the hunter comes back to the center and sees you, you already have 500-700 charges. Nice.
-COMBAT
~General
Steel Skin prayer (level 27) is great if you don't want people to know you're praying. +15% defense is nice.
~Prayer Swap
People with 43+ prayer generally use this. They swap protection prayers to match your combat type. Simple, not can be uneffective if your hunter knows how to fool you.
~Armor Swap
Less effective and harder than prayer swap, but useful if you have under 37 prayer. Bring all 3 types of armor in with you and change armors quickly according to opponent's combat style
~Rock-A-Run (40+ prayer required)
Annoying to hunters, but it works. Find a rock near the center, turn on Protection from Mage or Range, and run to where a melee opponent can't hit you. When they come around the rock to attack, run to the other side. Keep doing this.

If you play in F2P, the only items needed in FoG are a bow, arrows, and a melee weapon (scim, 2h, or grav). You want to avoid armor, as the weight will hinder you if your opponent runs or house hugs. If you must bring armor, don't wear it while attacking, as it will limit your effectiveness when switching combat styles.

Hunted tactics: Stay as close to the middle as possible. If your opponent is meleeing, use the two rocks in the middle of the arena as obstacles to put between your oppoenent and yourself. If they mage or range, put the portal houses between you and your oppoenent. House hugging is difficult to get the hang of at first, but certainly changes the game in your favor. Hiding is pretty much useless, because it is not that hard to find someone in such a small space. If you must hide, use the rocks at the north of the arena, because there you will get 10 charges per charge.

Hunter tactics: As soon as the game starts, teleport to the middle, and run to the opposite side of where you started. This prevents your opponent from hiding, and sometimes you can get a few hard hits on them before they pray. Keep switching styles of attacks, to get past an opponent with prayer. Due to how easy it is to rock-hug, try to avoid depending on melee. A range level of 55-60 is all you need for effective hits, as well as decent mage (47+). Try to switch attack styles every 2-4 hits, unless your opponent hasn't switched prayers.
