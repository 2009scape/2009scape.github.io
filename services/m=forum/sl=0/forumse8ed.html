-1.3-
As with any roleplaying clan, you are expected to follow a set of rules. If you consider some aspect of a rule to be "unfair," or if you think a rule should be removed/changed, please explain this to me so I can try to fix it.
1. Respect fellow clan members
2. no godmodding
3. no powerplaying
4. no metagaming
5. no spamming
6. no trolling
7. no autohitting
8. no bigotry
9. only use OOC (out of Character) in roleplay when bracketing it ((using double symbols like this.))
10. Follow the jagex rules

-3- Storyline

Twas early in the fifth age, when gielinor had finally come together into four main governments: The Imperial Cities, The Zaros Kale or Blood Sands, the Spectre Luna or Blue Moon Clan, and the Opheren. Each of these groups has their own unique characteristics along with government goals. However, soon all will break lose as gielinor is shattered, and these great governments become ruins, run by the remaining people...

Zaros Kale
The people in this group are the Main Antagonists. They are lead by Blood Priest Kale, who claims he was commanded by Zaros to kill anyone who was not part of his people. the Zaros Kale are based around the kharidian desert area. So the people of this government are sent on tasks to eliminate life, ranging from a single person to an entire city. join this group if you enjoy hardcore, constant fight roleplaying.
Benefits to joining Zaros Kale/Blood Sands: Access to a range of warfare magic for your character, based around the Ancient Magiks.
Downside: Lead by oppresive ruler, less detailed story line.

Spectre Luna
The people of this group are a wise, not often war faring people. They were at one time known as the moon clan, but would later join with the fremmenik and expand into the main land, forming the Spectre Luna. This group has remained safe from the Zaros Kale bombardments for some time, however have been preparing for an assault. As well, this group has many nomadic traveling caravans who were sent to use their magic to help people ravaged by the war. However, their travels are about to become more incredible than they could imagine. Characters in this group are considered part of an adventure based group. join this if you more so enjoy all around (travel/fight/storyline) roleplaying.
Benefits of joining: Access to a range of useful magic for your character, based around lunar magics.
Downside: Less chance of survival due to traveling during the Great War

Opheren
These people were established out of the major magic establishments, such as the Mages guild and the Mages tower. Although they have never shown any acts of warfare, they are greatly obsessed with power and, particularly, wealth. For the people who need their magical assistance, they charge ridiculous fees. Although nobody has proved it, there are rumors they actually capture people and turn them to gold. The Orpheren Octahedron (reigning 8 leaders) have called out for specific special jobs of their people, which will soon turn into much trouble for them. Characters in this group will mostly be doing talk sessions and fights.
Benefits of joining: Access to a range of magic similar to the Modern Magics.
Downside: Not much story line until late in Kingdom Fall, Considered "thieves" and enemies of many factions.

The Imperial Cities
This is the largest group. When the kingdoms split, all mages gathered and all ungifted people gathered in the main cities. People in the Imperial Cities are not mages, but rather a variety of both crafts and races. They are, however, because of their major city control, most targeted by the Zaros Kale. The emperor has recently called for the creation of a group specially to oppose the Zaros Kale. Characters in this group will be similar to the Spectre Luna (but without the magics) being equal in all aspects of roleplaying.
Benefits of joining: More freedom of non-mage characters, involvement in the "Major storyline."
Downside: Chance of death fairly high, due to being a group specifically involved in the war.

� Lawful Good � "Saintly" "Crusader"
�These characters lean toward righteousness and honor. They will usually help out of the goodness of their hearts. However, they struggle with either following the rules or breaking them to do good.
�Paladins, righteous knights...

� Neutral Good � "Benefactor"
�These characters act altruistically, regardless of a person's faction. They have some respect for others' rules and regulations, but they do not have much trouble moving around the rules to help others.
�Healers that help both sides of a battle...

� Chaotic Good � "Beatific" "Rebel" "Cynic"
�Obsessed with freedom, these characters shun ruling bodies that hinder improvement. They have no problem breaking rules or harming those they consider as "bad" people, as long as they reach their goal of the greater good.
�Rebels...

� Lawful Neutral � "Judge" "Disciplined"
�These characters are not torn between struggles of goodness or badness. Rather, they hold onto a set of rules or traditions and believe in honor and order.
�Obedient soldiers, judges...
� Neutral � "Undecided" "Nature's Alignment"
�These characters are not necessarily good or evil, nor lawful or chaotic. They do not lean toward either side and are either concerned with their own world or concerned with the balance. Either that, or they just lack any moral judgment.

�These characters do what they want to; rules have no meaning. Good and evil do not hold any light in the face of personal freedom, which is very important to these characters. They generally act for themselves, though not actually wishing to cause harm, and are unreliable.
