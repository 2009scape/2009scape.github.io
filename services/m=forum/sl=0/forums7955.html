Refuted Pros of BXPW
------------------------------

Bonus XP Weekend isn't really the 'end of the world', regardless of how much I hate it there are still some good benefits...

- Less Grinding

Yeah, some people seem to hate grinding. It makes me wonder why they decided to play RS in the first place, but those people do indeed exist. This may cut their pain of how much they hate grinding slightly, which may make the game funner for them.

However, I do think that more content that has less grind (and not a distraction & diversion) could easily remedy such an issue.

- More Members

Yup, we will get more F2Pers that will become a member, which results in more money for Jagex. That's always good, right? I suppose... until they get bored and quit anyway.

RWT was a big problem to Jagex because most people could just buy GP and 'complete' the game quicker, meaning that players would quit faster and result in less money for Jagex. Isn't it the same concept here?

They are a business after all and they do need money, and yes they will get a few legit members, but is it really worth it?

- Fixes Economy

This is probably the best argument for it, yes it does indeed make the economy a little better (after an initial shock to the prices). GP will leave the economy because people will train the money-draining skills, but so what?

To Jagex, simple shifts in the economy is like "saying that taking home a bucket of sand from the beach will make it disappear. The amount of GP that enter and leave the game on a daily basis is about 8 to 10 times as high as the amount that entered the game�s economy yesterday by increasing the value of the climbing boots."

- HAPPINESS

Everyone loves free XP! Does that mean it's right? Nope. There are legitimate concerns that do affect BXPW being right/wrong, so please do take a look the other side of things besides just "FREE XP FTW"

CONS OF BONUS XP WEEKEND
----------------------------------------------

With something as game changing as this, there are still bad sides to it which *MUST* be considered before a future event like this is done.

To the supporters: Please read this part, I know some of you are just excited about the whole thing and only see the Free XP part of it. I'm sure you may come out with a different opinion towards it after reading it...

- BXPWScape

Why train now when you can train on BXPW? We're all smart people, if you know that doing something now will earn you less XP then doing something later, you'd obviously wait a bit longer and get more XP. That's what's happening here.

I can safely say that *MOST* (and I don't usually like to pull out figures like that) people haven't been training the skills they plan to train on BXPW. This is WRONG. Players should *not* have to wait until a specific weekend so they can earn some more XP. It worked the first time because.. it was the first time.

Once the frequency of these events increase, people will just hoard items until the event occurs. What would become of RS when nobody wishes to train except on BXPW? This won't become a massive issue until more events like these are made.

- GE Prices

Some of this is actually a pro. Getting GP out of the game is always a good thing, and giving the economy a little 'boost' is okay... until it gets too far. I don't think the prices that I saw on the BXPW were justified to their real values.

However, this isn't a huge issue as prices usually return back to their prices afterwards. For how long, though? It seems that every time a rumour pops up about BXPW, the economy starts freaking out.

CONS OF BXPW CONTINUED
------------------------------------------

- Elite content

Some of the content I love playing involve bosses, I think they're awesome. God Wars, Tormented Demons, Corporeal beast, etc. To become better at those specific bosses you often have to seek new advantages, such as Turmoil Prayers, Overloads, and better familiars.

Often times, they are hard to get. No, they *ARE* hard to get. Those skills are expensive for a reason, they are very powerful! They are the same reason why you removed them from PVP (Overloads), the same reason why familiars don't have access to all areas!

What you did now is come along and make a lot of people get these achievements much easier (exact figures few posts below) to get. I have no problem with people getting these achievements, but I do have a problem when it makes content easier.

Let me explain in the simplest of terms.

More people obtaining better advantages = More people killing bosses faster and easier.

More bosses killed faster and easier = More drops per hour.

More drops per hour = More rare drops per hour.

More rare drops per hour = Price drop! Suddenly items like Dragon claws become more common because you're able to kill them faster.

Do you want proof? Fine. Send your mod account to kill a Tormented Demon with Overloads and Steel Titan. The kill should not take you longer than a minute. Now the developers at Jagex aren't stupid, they know this, that's why they SET high requirements for this!

So *WHY* do you insist on making it easier to get?! You're just falling in your own traps! You make hard requirements then decide to give everyone a very big boost into getting these, eliminating all the balancing you've put into the game.

So PLEASE don't hand this powerful content out like candy, it's seriously going to make the game easier!

I believe that most PVMers are against the BXPW, but unfortunately not every one kills bosses so nobody seems to care about this legit concern

SOLUTIONS
-----------------

I have had lots of cool ideas and I have seen some epic ideas on the other thread. Maybe Jagex can do it differently this time?

Solution #1 - XP Cap

I think it is a bit overpowered to gain millions of XP in a short span of time, it seems like a bug more than anything (when it's not). I propose to cap the amount of bonus XP you can get from each skill.

Buyable skills can have a limit that increases as you get higher into the skill, so at about level 90 or so, the maximum XP you'd get would be approx 500K.

Production skills should have the same as above.

Character development skills should have no limit.

If that's too complicated, then just set 500K xp to each skill MAX.

Solution #2 - Remove Buyables

Buyables seem to hold the most concern towards the event, so maybe you can just disable them like you did with Prayer? You can even combine it with Solution #1

Solution #3 - Do other weekends!

Not everything on RS is just about XP, there's lots of cool ideas that you can give a similar concept to...

~ Distraction and Diversions Weekend

On that weekend, you would gain extra penguin points for spying on a penguin, the stardust cap would be increased to 300, faster starting times for Fish Flingers and the game limit increased, Evil Trees and Familiarization would start faster, and offer more generous rewards, the Phoenix Lair would have better changes of receiving a phoenix egg, and an increased chance of getting a shattered heart's rock/ancient effigy.

D&D's aren't really played by many, so if you can give more people a slight push to play them with temporarily increased rewards, it would be pretty generous and wouldn't hurt a thing!

Solution #4 - Skill Themes

Perhaps every few month, a few skills can be drawn from a hat and those skills would feature on the next BXPW. This would mean the bonus xp only affects those skills. Of course, limits would need to be applied, etc.
