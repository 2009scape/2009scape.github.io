The Main Ladder has been around for a very long time, and has developed well due to the unwritten aspect of its constitution. Though people may hold the thread more than once (indeed, I�ve held it numerous times), it is convention that they don�t hold it in immediate succession.

Part 4 � The History of the Ladder

The Main Ladder is the oldest and most tradition in RuneScape, far surpassing other organised schemes of warring in any platform; be they on the forums or off them. Founded by Lady Foul several years ago, the ladder was originally designed to legislate Clan warfare on a prearranged level and promote disciplined warring on a wilderness basis. Most of the legislation not concerned with the field of battle and mechanisms that have changed over the years remains relatively similar to the rules typed up ages ago.

Upon the death of the wilderness and the resignation of Lady Foul as threadholder there were elections for a Ladder Council, where some of the original founders were voted in along with newer Clan Leaders. Legislation in regards to the rapidly changing environment of war (due to the archaic legislation and it�s now non-existent origin) was slow and admonishments were sporadic, eventually I as a councilmember resigned and had the council removed through a community vote.

The system of government was restored to that outlined above in part 3 where a rotational threadholder would serve as the executive for the month. Eventually I decided with necessary Clan support to reform major parts of the legislation, rearrange it and introduce new disciplinary guidelines to help with retaining law and order. Soon afterwards, the Horae was introduced which most ladders base their admonishments systems on today.
