First 4 Hours


Hi guys,

We'd love to hear what you think of the beginning of the Runescape experience; what you like or dislike, what you would like to see or feel is really not needed, maybe even ideas on how new players could be helped to get through those early stages and get a real feel and love for the game.

So if you feel you have got anything to contribute, please post it here. I will be regularly reading and posting on this thread and will ensure all good feedback/suggestions are highlighted for consideration.

Heya.

I started back in 2005 and my tutorial was the tutorial island. I've tried all the new tutorials released after that, and I've to admit the newer tutorials were "cool", but still not so useful.

The old tutorial island taught me how to train most of my skills and how to fight, in a very good way. It didn't also have too many flashing arrows and was still pretty straightforward.

The tutorial with killing the dragon was cool, but kind of useless as new players won't be killing dragons for quite a few levels, and also it didn't even show the real way of killing dragons.

I also prefer the old tutorial island over the advisor system. The advisor system got really annoying, I don't want to click on all the time when I get a bone, am low hp, pick up meat etc.

What I think should be done is promoting the game manual more! I still use it a lot, and it is so helpful and interesting to read. I try to point people to the knowledge base when they have some more complex questions about skills or areas, and I wish players would be introduced to the game manual in-game aswell, preferably in the tutorial.

To be honest, I'd like the old tutorial island back, it was the best by far. Only thing it was missing is introducing game manual (which didn't exist back then), and a brief introduction to how you can make a bit of money (like cowhides, feathers or other basic ways).

The first four hours of game play are really what you make of it ... literally. the only thing that helps you through the game in those beginning stages is your interest in the game. it all stems back to whether the tutorial made you wish to pursue the game. did the tutorial interest you enough to continue playing for an additional four hours?

I've been a member for many years and have just now tried the demo. all in all a better introduction to runescape than tutorial island in my opinion. some bits however were lacking. as this game is almost built around running around and gaining levels, and at the same time gaining a sense of accomplishment for gaining those levels, i was disappointed to see that i got no fireworks for leveling my mining in the demo. i feel gaining that sense of accomplishment is really what hooks players into the game in the beginning, and i don't think that's really achieved when your character levels up and nothing happens. Of coarse as you play more frequently your reasons for playing change as you explore the game, but in the tutorial leveling should be emphasized a bit more

also mentioned above about tutorial island, we did have a very intense fight with a level 3 rat. with this new demo there's no "fight for your life" feeling. i think that aspect of the tutorial should be restored to some degree in the demo because the goblin just didn't cut it

the only other aspect of the demo i didn't like was the fact that players were denied the experience of other combat styles. i think range and mage are to complicated to introduce to a character flooded with new information and a new interface, but at least being able to see the other forms of combat in action would make the game more attractive. perhaps also adding 2 more npc* that range and mage that die off with vant's other companion

runescape is a very attractive free game as it stands now, that's why it's number one. these are just some things i've noticed that may be lacking.
