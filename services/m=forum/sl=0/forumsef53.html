Carefully reread rules V and SeVen if you want your character to be a Mage. And if not, reread them just for the heck of it.

So. This is just a general overview of a Mage�s abilities, but I�ll leave a lot up in the air so that you all have the chance to shape magic as you will (within reason, that is).

To begin with, a Mage�s power is determined not by age but by experience and dedication to master the wild magic. Magic, it its rawest form, is all around us. It is in the very air, and only certain gifted people have the ability to recognize magic for what it is, and to harness or tame it for their own uses.

When a Mage reaches his mid to late teens, his abilities become manifest, though in is not uncommon for his talents to become clear at a later time. If not properly trained, a Mage is something like a magnet to wild magic, which will use the Mage as a conduit for its own uses. This can become very hazardous to those around the Mage, as well as the Mage himself. Due to this, young, aspiring Magi are required to appear before the Council so that a suitable master can be found to train the Mage and aid him in his quest to master control over his gift. A master will usually begin teaching an apprentice how to correctly reach for the flows of magic, harness, and either use or dissipate it.

It is this control that distinguishes a trained Mage from an untrained Mage, for an untrained Mage is controlled and used by his magic.

Obviously, a Mage�s strength, self-discipline, and experience determine what tasks his magic can accomplish.
