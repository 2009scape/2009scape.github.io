I like the new additions to lower level players.

However, I personally believe that changes need to be made to the equipment that lower level players use. What I mean by this is this:

I see many low level players weilding:
-Scimitars
-2h's
-Sometimes battleaxes

I only see the very new ones weilding:
-Swords
-Daggers
-Longswords

I don't really see anyone weilding:
-Maces
-Warhammers
-Any members low level equipment item (claws, halberds, spears)

The reason why, is because some pieces of equipment really aren't useful. Scimitars pull the best damage per second of any of those other weapons, and 2hs give a nice strength bonus and attack stats against higher level monsters.

Now, most lower level players don't know much of that, but they will eventually learn that much of the toys they can create with smithing are not as useful as they thought. And they cannot use many of the members items anyway.

I suggest making all the weapons of lower-level pieces of equipment useful. They should be equal, but still different to allow players to choose what they like. Not only that, but I do think there should be some diversity in combat when it comes to armor and weapons. Chainbodies and platebodies should be equal in how good they are, but chainbodies should defend from crush more than plate, and plate should defend from stab more than chain. They should be the same price, require the same amount of bars, and give the same slash bonus.

This would create more strategy in low level combat. If you had a sword as a weapon, you would have the upperhand against someone in chainmail. But if you had a warhammer, you would have a disadvantage against someone in chain.

Some of these weapons should be worked with and updated.

I will also honestly say, that I suggest you make some members low level equipment be for free players. Members don't really use halberds, or claws, or spears, and this could be a great addition to the free game.

Here's my first four hours.

I came to Runescape and did the tutorial, trying to get my bearings. I learned how to mine, Wc, Fm, fish, cook, and some combat.

I came off at Lumbridge and started to walk around. The first thing I killed was an imp scurrying around. It was imposing to see so many people with red numbers, but I actually made friends with one of them and they showed me around lumbridge.

He then left and I went on to explore. I went around and found myself in Al-Kharid's mine, where I died for the first time, at the hands of a scorpion. I was upset and confused but I stuck with it, that was almost five years ago now!

I also recently created a new account. I played through the new tutorial and was agitated with it. I learned nothing. I was told exactly what to do for a quest, but honestly, quests are something you learn to figure out, not something that need to be explained to you, like skills. I left the tutorial with the knowledge of.. how to chop wall roots.

I don't need to learn to slay a dragon at lvl 3. I need to know how to kill a rat or a goblin and where to get my levels up at. That's what I think, at least.

Honestly, if you put the old and new tutorial together and strung them together with, "well now that you have the skills to survive in runescape, maybe you can help out our white knight friend... he seems to need your help" and the wizard tele's you into the new tutorial, right from tutorial island.
