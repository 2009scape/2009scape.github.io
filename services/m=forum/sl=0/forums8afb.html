Frequently Asked Questions:

Q:"I don't have a high enough level, but my friend does! May I use the 'Request Assistance' feature to complete the task?"

A: I'm sorry, but you must have achieved the level yourself. Using this feature will NOT count towards your Achievement Diary.

Q: "May I use stat boosting items such as an Admiral Pie? It would save me lots of time. "

A: Yes. You may use items which temporarily boost your level in a skill in order to complete most tasks. If you find a task for which this doesn't work, if you could please post it, I would gladly make a note saying so, preventing others from wasting their time trying.

Q: "You said I needed 33 Quest Points, but the Legends' Quest says 108!! You're wrong."

A: I'm afraid that I have included only the minimum requirements to do the Achievement Diaries in this thread. If you do not have a quest that is listed completed, I would suggest checking out that quest's page in the Game Guide. The minimum requirements for each quest are given in that page.

Q: "A new Achievement Diary came out today! Can I make the list for you?"

A: While I appreciate that you wish to help, and I really am thankful for the sheer zeal that you are willing to display, I must ask that nobody posts a list of minimum requirements or rewards on this thread until at least twenty four hours after the diary has been released. This ensures that all users can experience the update how they wish.

"About boosts: if you have at least started the Evil Dave part of Recipe for Disaster quest, you may use coloured spices to boost your skills up to +6, although a +6 boost is EXTREMALLY rare! So, I suggest you to only use those for +5 boosts. Note: for skills that already have other ways to boost +5, like agility (summer pie), don't use the stews.

Hints for stews:

1. You can buy [ten] stews on the Warrior's Guild for only 100 gp each.

2. Complete Rat Catchers to get a Wily cat, than stock up with lots of spices. No need to keep red ones, as the combat boosts of those is ridiculous compared to the normal potions.

3. Only keep full bottles. Bottles with 1, 2 ou 3 bits can be used on eachother to get full bottles. Drop the empty ones. This will save bank space.

4. When going to boost a skill, always use 3 doses of the same spice on the stew (if you want a +5 boost). Take a handful of stews and spices and alternative boosts. The boost cxan also be negative. If your skill is decreased, you should only eat another bowl when your skill is back to the regular level. This is where the alternative boost comes. Optionally, you can just put Rapid Heal prayer or Dream spell. Everytime you eat the thing, quickly check the Stats screen, and if you got the wanted level, just do the action as fast as possible.

Spices:

Red (Combat): Attack, Strength, Defence, Ranged and Magic
Yellow (Mental): Prayer, Agility, Slayer, Thieving and Hunter
Brown (Earth): Woodcutting, Herblore, Farming, Fishing and Mining
Orange (Manual): Smithing, Crafting, Fletching, Runecrafting, Cooking, Construction and Firemaking

Of those, the red is almost useless (only worth to get a +5 magic boost), and brown and orange are the most useful, specially for quests and Achievement Diaries boosts."

The above quote is courtesy of Rafael Lobo. It is a direct quote, and all information and spellings are his, and not mine.
