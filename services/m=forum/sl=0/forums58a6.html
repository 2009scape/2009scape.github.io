.:: HONOR CODE ::.

Definition of Honor:

Good name or public esteem : REPUTATION

Examples of no honor:


1. Mass Recruitment

- Recruiting individuals to join your clan for the soul purpose of warring in a single war and not for long term retention.

Example:
Setting up a location at Pest Control or GE and spamming "Join The Great Valor 108+!"

If proven this will result in punishment by the council to be decided by the threadholder on an individual basis from anti immunity to suspension from the ladder for 90 days.


2. Excessive Flaming

- As defined on page two of this thread under Flaming.


3. Unlisted/Multiclan Members

- Unlisted:
Bringing individuals to a war that are NOT posted on your members list and their only purpose is to fight in a war.
If someone is in your cc and in the same world as the war they will be assumed to be in your clan regardless if they are on the wall or fighting in the war.

Any spelling errors, #name changes or multiple name changes will be considered unlisted. If it is a single name change the ref will check this and post the list after.

If a clan brings more than 5 unlisted it is and automatic disqualification and if more than 10 then additionally 15 days of anti immunit. If repeated 3 times the clan will be kicked for 30 days from the ladder.

Each unlisted will not be allowed to war in the next war you have and you will receive one day anti immunity for each unlisted up to the maximum of 5 as per the above statement.

Multi-clann(ing)

"You are not allowed to represent more than one clan on the RSB Ladder Rankings or the RSB Battle Ladder.

KEY WORDS: Not active in warring on the ladders


4. Crashing another Clan's War

- Simple as crashing another clans Planned fight.
Example: TGV vs TRS 20v20 matched PvP, FDR crash while they are in the middle of the war.
Examples:
Standing on the wall and flaming either clan or ref.
Entering a clans cc without thier permission

.:: General Ladder Rules ::.

These rules apply to all leagues however additional rules are in place for each league.
Rules cover Ladder and declaration threads.

01) All Jagex rules.


02) No Multi-Claning. (see honor code for definition)


03) No Flaming. Flaming of any sort will be dealt with using the Grade system (see flaming section page 2)


04) You may declare on any Clan ahead of you in ranks.


05) You have 3 days to accept or decline a War, after being informed.

Once accepted you have 3 days to complete final negotiations and set date of war.
If negotiations are not finalized then the default rules will be utalized and the threadholder will set date and time.

Date of war cannot exceed 14 days from the date the war is confirmed.
This gives you a Maximum of 17 days from day war is declared. 3 for declaration and 14 for The war to take place.


06) A Clan under Immunity may decline without penalty.
Temporary immunity starts when a declaration is made for the declared upon clan.
The declared upon clan is immune from they day they are declared on plus 5 days more after the war is fought.
If you are dq or decline you lose immunity.


07) You may not declare, if you are in Anti-Immunity.


08) Accurate Members list must be posted 24 hours after the war is accepted in alphabetical order.

Any spelling errors, #name changes or multiple name changes will be considered unlisted. Single name change to be ckecked by ref.

Minimum of 24 hours prior to war if not posted then the clan will forfeit round 1.
(Note must be requested and both parties notified prior to war)


09) The Ladder is owned by the RBL Council
(See the "Power" section further into the Thread)
If you are a leader of an active Battle Ladder clan then you may motion to the council for review of a rule or ruling.


10. All cheating in a War should be posted with evidence on the results thread.


11. Unless posted on the dec. thread attack/defend and potting notices are not required

.:: Declining a declaration ::.

If a clan declines a war they will lose 2 ranks.

If a clan declines 3 wars in a row they will be removed from the league.

Note: These are based on individual leagues and not the entire ladder so they will not be penalized on all leagues.

.:: Failure to reply to a declaration ::.

If a clan fails to reply to a declaration they will lose 4 ranks.

If a clan fails to respond to 2 wars in a row they will be removed from the league.

.:: Failure to show for a war or withdraw from a war after completing negotiations ::.

First incident:
The standard punnishment for this is the same as if they never responded with additional notes.

1. lose 4 ranks
2. anti immunity for 15

That is the standard punnishment for the RSBL for such actions.

Second incident:

1. dropped to bottom of rankings
2. anti immunity for 30 days

Third incident:

removed from league for 30 days

.:: Immunity and Anti-Immunity ::.

.: Immunity :.

Immunity is defined as a clan is able to decline your declaration if they so please without risk to their rank. It is given whenever you have been declared on, and when you have fought a War.

The declaring clan does not gain immunity at any time.

When you have been declared upon, you gain Immunity, this lasts until such time as you have completed warring or you forfeit the war. When you have fought a War, the clan declared on gains 5 days Immunity, regardless of whether you won or lost. In addition if your clan declines or fails to respond to a war even if you are in immunity you will forfeit your rank. You must respond stating you decline due to immunity. If your forfeit or fail to accept the war Anti-Immunity will take place. Wars are declared and negotiated on a first declaration basis.


.: Anti-Immunity :.

Anti-Immunity is the opposite of Immunity. When you are in Anti-Immune, you cannot declare on any clan for the length of the Anti-Immunity, but can be declared upon and must accept all wars or you forfeit your rank.

Anti-Immunity also cancels out all Immunity you previously had. For example, you had three days Immunity, then are given ten days Anti-Immunity. This means that you now have no Immunity, and ten days worth of Anti-Immunity.

Anti-Immunity is given in three ways;

Punishment - See the other rules as to what can make you have Anti-Immunity as a punishment.
If you bring unlisted or multi clanners to a war see definition in the Honor code section of this thread.
Flaming or breaking the Honor code.

Forfeiting/Declining - If you forfeit a declaration or fail to respond to the declaration the opposing Clan made, then you are given five days Anti-Immunity.

Disqualification - If a clan is disqualified then they will receive anti-immunity but the amount of such will be decided based on the severity of their disqualification.
A minimum of 5 days will be issued.
