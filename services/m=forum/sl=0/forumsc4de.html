DNA and Reproduction

Key Concepts of Life

DNA is the genetic information that makes up you. It is a sequence of nucleotides. It is translated into a long chain of proteins and folded into the physical life you are.

=== As***** Reproduction ===
Creation of *GENETICALLY IDENTICAL* offspring is made by the LONE parent. Budding, binary fission, ect...

=== S***** Reproduction ===
Creation of *GENETICALLY VARIATING* offspring is made by two contributing parents. 50% of the genetic information from each.
Humans do this.

=== Mutations ===
A change in nucleotide sequence of an organism's DNA. DNA is copied wrong during replication. It can be substituted or deleted accordingly. The greatest source of genetic diversity.

All mutations belong to one of the following groups:
Adapative - helps an organism survive longer to reproduce more.
Silent - doesn't help or hinder.
Mal-adapative - hinders survival and reproductive success.

=== Causes of Mutations ===
UV rays
Radioactivity
(Smoke?)

Key Terms, Understanding, & Observations

=== Species ===
A group whose members possess similiar anatomical characteristics and have the ability to interbreed and produce FERTILE OFFSPRING.
Horse + Donkey makes a mule, but the mule cannot reproduce with either of a another mule. Horse/Donkeys are different, though distantly related, species. Mules are subspecies.

=== Population ===
A group of individuals of one species and living in the same geographic area.

===== Observation #1 =====
Individuals in a population vary in many heritable traits

===== Observation #2 =====
A population of any species has the potential produce far more offspring than that will survive to produce offspring of their own.

Microevolution

The process takes less time and does not make new species.

======= Natural Selection =======
A process in which organisms with certain inheritable characteristics are likely to survive and reproduce than organisms with other characteristics.

======= Genetic Drift (aka Random Chance Selection) =======
The change in the gene pool due to to chance. Events such as hurricanes, floods, or fires can kill large numbers of a population and change the allele frequency. These events are called the bottleneck effect. Its random on what survives.

======= Gene Flow =======
Where a population gain or lose alleles when fertile individuals move into or out of a population or when gametes are transferred between populations. (refer to the post above) This can keep populations 'looking' the same.

NOTE: Of the three types of microevolution, only natural selection makes a population adaptive to its environment.

Macroevolution

Evolutionary change on a grand scale, encompassing the origin of new taxonomic groups, evolutionary trends, adaptive readiation, and mass extinction.

======= The Causes of New Species, Biodiversity, and Speciation =======
Temporal Isolation - mating seasons at different times.
Habitat Isolation - You don't live near them.
Behavioral Isolation - No attraction between them.
Mechanical Isolation - It just can't be done.
Gametic Isolation - Gametes can't unite.

======= Speciation, the Emergence of a New Species =======
This occurs when a population of one species 'split' into geographically isolated areas. Once geographically isolated, the two groups of population will face different types of environments - which will cause different types of adapations to prevail.

Random *DIFFERENT* mutations may occur in each population and benefit them based on the mechanics of microevolution. All different populations will continue to reproduce and after enough generations, will not be able to reproduce with the other populations. They have the same ancestors, but are seperate species.

The 'Everstone' of Life

In order for a population to not be evolving, it must satify the principle of Hardy-Weinberg equilibrium:

1. Very large population
2. No gene flow
3. No mutations
4. Random mating
5. No natural selection

===== The reasoning =====
1. "The smaller the population, the more likely it is that allele frequencies will fluctuate by chance from one generation the the next."
2. "When individuals move into or out of populations, they add or remove alleles."
3. "By altering alleles or deleting or duplicating entire genes, mutations modify the gene pool."
4. "If individuals mate peferentially, such as with close relatives, random mixing of gametes do not occur, and genotype frequencies change. Allele frequencies, however, usually tend to stay the same."
5. "The differential survival and reproductive success of individuals (natural selection) can alter frequencies."

Naturally, it is very unlikely for a species to be 'not evolving.' It is possible, of course, but as much as I can tell, it mostly occurs with artificial interference (artificial selection). (Ever wonder how man's best friend came to be or how Orville got those popcorn to be large?)

The Perfect Species

There are 4 reasons that life cannot be the 'perfect species':

1. Selection can act only on existing variations.
2. Evolution is limited by historical restraints.
3. Adaptations are often compromises.
4. Chance, natural selection, and the environment interact.

=== The Reasoning ===
1. New advantageous alleles do not come into to play because its demanded. It only acts on what it has, whats already existing.
2. Evolution works on what the organisms already have. It doesn't just add on adaptations on the ancestral anatomy. It adapts what is already has to the environment.
3. Each organism must do different things. If a human where to grow wings where there were arms, they won't have those hands.
4. Chance affects the genetic structure of populations more than once believed. In a bottlenecking event, not always will the better suited for the environment make it.
