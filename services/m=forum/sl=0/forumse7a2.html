16-Jul-2010 19:00:22
Of all the above methods, only one of them yields a profit, and none are as fast as my suggestions. Training Magic with combat is the best way to go-

I suggest either Aviansies or Blue dragons-both will yield a profit if you use the wave or even the surge spells (though surge spells will only yield profit if you use a staff of light with battle robes).

If you decide to try Aviansies, bring Void Mage armour (unless you have high defence) because Void Mage is the only set of magic robes that gives *any* ranged defence. Bones to peaches is suggested if you want to stay for long periods of time, but an inventory of sharks actually lasts quite a while. If you use fire wave you *WILL* make a profit!

If you decide to try blue dragons, you can bring your favourite set of robes (battle robes being the best). Contrary to Jagex's claims that magic is inaccurate on dragons, fire wave actually hits very well on blue dragons-and the surge spells hit incredibly hard. The Ogre enclave is the best place to kill blue dragons-however, it requires the Watchtower quest. Seeing as you are looking to train magic, and since the Watchtower quest gives 12,000 Magic exp, I strongly reccomend doing it for access to the best dungeon for blue dragons. There are very few people in the dungeon- you will almost alway have 2 dragons to yourself.

While this method yields more profit than the Aviansie method, you will be spending time banking and collecting bones/hides and therefor gain less exp/hour.


Both of these methods will make you money! Aviansies drop four noted adamant bars nearly every four kills, which is more than enough to pay for your runes-and leaving you with a bit of extra money leftover. The same goes for blue dragons-they drop dragon bones (~6,000 gp) and blue dragonhide (~3,000 gp) EVERY TIME!

Hope this helps-
Smiley Frog8 (currently level 93 in Magic)
