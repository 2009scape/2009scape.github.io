Mrschrystler


After a week of playing RuneScape, while mining in Al Kharid, a friend of mine pointed out that there was a "mining camp" in the southern desert. He said he was going to go check it out (we thought it might be another guild). Five minutes later he pms me saying "I can't get there; it�s in a members-only area!"

I told him not to worry, because when my husband got home from work I would sign up for members and go check it out to let him know what it was.

So,needless to say, Hubby comes home, I sign up for members. I go to Shantay... I buy new pretty white robes, and boots... buy a pass and go into the desert. I start wandering around and finally come to the gate of the "mining camp" only to find out that I can't get in because it�s for a quest.

So I start heading back to Shantay only to get lost, and start dying of thirst.

When I finally get back to Shantay Pass, I die right before I get out of the desert losing all my money and my pretty new robes.

I can't remember the exact level I was, other than mid 20s.

Oh the joys of being new to a game!

Catmania29


When I first joined RuneScape, I knew little to nothing about clans or the impact that they had on the RuneScape community. At that point, I could have been described as a �noob�, not even knowing what �lol� stood for. As I stumbled my way through RuneScape, I slowly but steadily began to understand the meanings behind what people said, how to train my skills, and how to communicate properly with other players. When I discovered the RuneScape forums, my whole mindset regarding RuneScape was revolutionised.

The only clans I had ever been a part of before this discovery were merely short-lived groups that me and my siblings came up with. As I roamed the forums, I eventually joined a clan, left it, and moved on to another one. In each of the clans I joined, I formed my own opinions on what I saw, how I could make some of the problems that they faced easier, and things that could be improved upon.

This was how I made my way to where I find myself today. After hopping from clan to clan, I had come to the conclusion that my opinions were so unique, that I would have to start my own clan if I wanted to have rules that I knew I could follow. I spent several days concocting a plan and a general idea for the clan I had in mind. I made a thread, set up rules, elected someone to lead the clan beside me, and the clan was on its way to becoming what it is today.

There are many clans in RuneScape, each with their own set of rules and ideals. I suppose that the Skillers of the Round Table are one of the more laid-back clans � we have events, we have rules, but usually we don�t have to enforce those rules, and the events we have are not mandatory. Other clans might find it better to have mandatory events, especially if it�s a Distractions and Diversions clan. Different clans each find different things that fit their ideals � and we have found ours.

Some might say our clan is sort of random. When we enforce our rules, we try to enforce the meaning of the law rather than the word of the law. Our original goals in making this clan were to have a fun, safe environment where we could get together, chat, and skill together without too much strife. Because of this, we can come up with some very strange ideas, occasionally leading to interesting conversations about pastry wars. If you�d like to see what I mean, here�s a snippet of one particular conversation:

A: *throws a cookie at Q's face*
Q: Ow!
A: Oo
A: *throws another cookie at Q's face* Catch!
Q: *Throws biscuit at A*
Q: Ow!
C: Mayday! Mayday! Taking heavy pastry fire! Requesting evac!
Q: Fire the custard cannon!
Q: *bang!*
Q: *custard hits A*
A: [transmission lost]--pie tin shield--*splat!*
--[transmission lost]�

Yep. That about sums up our clan. We are the ever-random Skillers of the Round Table, and proud to call ourselves unusual.

But moving on, I realise that some people like to make clans just to be a leader. Although sometimes this might end up in the creation of an amazing and awesome clan, this is, many times, not the case. I suggest that, before creating a clan, you think about your motives. If they don�t fit the role required of being a clan leader, you should just join someone else�s clan and work your way up in rank. Joining someone else�s clan and gaining ranks there is probably one of the best ways to learn how to be a good leader. By taking it step by step, you learn more than you would have if you had made your own clan from the start.

It's time for our latest caption competition.

Your challenge is to write a funny caption that suits the image perfectly. We know you can do it!
