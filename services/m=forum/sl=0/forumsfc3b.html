Post 2: General how to not get hacked information

1) Passcode keeping
2) Recovery keeping
3) Scams!

======

*1*

Passcode keeping:

It is VERY important for you to update your passcode every month, or so. Here is an article by JaGeX in the Customer Support part of the website:

What is a good passcode?

A good passcode is one that is not easy to guess or work out, is easy for you to remember, is at least eight letters long and contains a mix of letters and numbers.

* DO NOT pick any word or number which has a connection to you, so don't pick your house number or street name, for example.
* DO NOT just use a number at the end of a word, such as HVYDG8076
* DO NOT use common number and letter substitutions e.g. 4 as the word 'for', 1 as 'L' or 'I', 5 as 'S'
* DO NOT use repeating characters 'bbbbbbbbbb' or series of characters such as 'kbkbkbkbkbkb'.
* DO NOT use your name, account name or name of an item either forwards, backwards or divided up.
* DO NOT use a series of characters off any keyboard such as 'qwerty', 'lkjhgf' or 'qazw**edc', as these are very common and hijackers will look for these.

======
*2*

Recovery keeping:

Make sure you DO create recovery questions. Questions such as:

What is your mothers maiden name?
What was your first pet's name?
What is your father's middle name?
What is the birthdate of your best friend?

Are the most common. Some of these are used by JaGeX during the set-up process, and are suggested to use. When setting up recoveries, remember to WRITE THEM DOWN and place them somewhere only *YOU* will find them. Example: your personal safe.

======
*3*

Scams!

PLEASE CHECK POST 6 FOR MORE DETAILS ON SCAMS.

Scams are found all over the place on RuneScape. Please DO NOT take part in any scams, as a PERMANENT ban will take place if you are caught. REPORT any scammers that you find or see, IMMEDIATELY.

Some of the most common scams are:
1) Acting like a JaGeX Mod
2) Saying they will "boost your account"

Post 3: Keep Your Computer Cleaned

I have a full thread written in the Tech Support forum, and this is a little tid-bit of that thread. If you like this further information on how to clean up your computer, please check out this QFC: 25-26-292-60779885.

======

The basics to keeping your computer cleaned is considered of the following:

1) Disk cleanup (or CCleaner)
2) Disk degfragmentation
3) Deleting old files

======
*1*


Start > All Programs > Accessories > System Tools > Disk Cleanup

Once this loads, click "Files From all Users on This Computer" and when prompted, click Continue. (if on Windows 7, skip this step)

After that, click on the C:/ drive, or your Hard Drive. (main drive, NOT memory)

DO NOT CLICK ON THE RAM, THIS WILL REMOVE NOTHING, AND WILL WASTE TIME

After this loads, another Box will appear:

Check off ALL of the boxes, PLEASE MAKE SURE THAT EVERYTHING IS IN THE RECYCLE BIN THAT YOU WANT TO REMOVE

After you do that, click OK and Delete Files.

======
*2*


Start > All Programs > Accessories > System Tools > Disk Defragmentor


Now, when you click on this, you will need to be prompted, so click on Continue. (or if it does not prompt, just skip to the next step)

After this loads, select a time and date (Weekly or Monthly), so it defragments while the computer is off. (or on)

Suggestion: every week during school hours!

After that, click DEFRAGMENT NOW.

Check both boxes, then click OK.

======
*3*

Cleaning up old files:

This is quite simple. Going into your documents and deleting old videos, songs, and even written documents can remove a lot of unusable space from your computer.

Just make sure everything you are deleting is YOUR documents, not your family's.

======

Why is this useful for protecting me from a hacker?

Well, first of all, this speeds up your computer's performance and it also helps your other programs run better on your computer. Example: Your virus protector.

Post 4: Account help!

There are many different account issues out there, such as:

1) Membership help
2) Electronic Mail Registration
3) Recovering Passcodes + Accounts
4) Appealing offenses
5) Billing issues
6) Reporting abuse

======

*1*

Membership help:

To start or end a current RuneScape subscription follow these directions:

1) RuneScape home page
2) Account (tab) > Account Management
3) Start/End Subscription

Start/End Subscription is found in the top, left hand corner of all of the options to pick from on the Account Management page.

======
*2*

Electronic Mail Registration:

1) RuneScape home page
2) Account (tab) > Account Management
3) Electronic Mail Registration

Please make sure you have a Mail Address that you will REMEMBER. It is HIGHLY recommended that you use one that you use for friends and family. (So that you know which mail address it is)

======
*3*

Recovering Passcodes and Accounts:

1) RuneScape home page
2) Account (tab) > Account Management
3) *SCROLL DOWN TO THE BOTTOM* Recover Account/Recover Poasscode

Please remember that a recovery can take a LONG time for any action to happen, especially if the account you are trying to recover is F2P.

======
*4*

Appealing offenses:


1) RuneScape home page
2) Account (tab) > Account Management
3) Appeal Offense

Please note that an offense appeal can take awhile to be responded to. JaGeX has to carefully investigate the matter and also take appropriate actions against, or for, the account.

======
*5*

Billing issues:


1) RuneScape home page
2) Help (tab) > Customer Support > Billing

If none of the topics there helps, submit a billing query at the bottom of the main billing page.

======
*6*

Reporting abuse:


1) RuneScape home page
2) Help (tab) > Customer Support > Reporting Abuse

That section of the site is very informative, but if you have a forum issue, please report it at these QFC's:
Forum Help: 103-104-16-61148848
Forum Emergencies: 103-104-17-61148852

Post 5: VIRUS FREE!

To keep your computer virus free, you are going to have to:

1) Use a safe browser
2) Use an updated Virus protector
3) Update your computer (Check Index for full post on this)

*1*

Use a safe browser:

There are many types of browsers out there, but using a SAFE one and one that is not either in BETA mode or one that is new is essential.

Suggested browsers by users and JaGeX:
*note: these are in NO way listed from best to least*

-Internet Explorer
-Firefox
-Safari
-Google Chrome
-Opera

===========

*2*

Use an updated virus protector

Using a virus protection product is very important while surfing the web, and it is almost as important that it is updated.

The reason why: Hackers and other people like that do not care about your computer's well being. Every so often, a new kind of virus, trojan or worm will be created, and the virus companies will do everything they can in their power to try and stop them from breaking the firewall to your computer. Having a virus protecting program will stop most of these viruses, and using a default Windows firewall will not.

Recommended Virus protecting products from users:
*note: these are in NO way listed from best to least*

-Norton AntiVirus 2010*
-Norton Internet Security*
-AVG 9.0
-McAfee 2010 Security Software*
-Avast!
-Kaspersky Internet Security (free trail)
-SandBoxie

* = Costs money per year

(If you have more Virus protectors that you find great and worth the while, please post them!)


Scanning the computer at a scheduled scan is recommended monthly. Make sure that the scan is set to when you think best. Also, make sure that it completes a 'FULL System Scan' every time. This will detect any viruses, trojans or worms that may have infected your computer.

===========

*3*

Update your computer:

Please check post number 8 for information on updating your computer.

Post 6 - Scams (in general)
___________________________________

With any major thing, there are, of course, those who want to benefit off the mistakes of others. Unfortunately, there are people who do this in Runescape, and we call them scammers.

What these scammers try to do is get you to do something that would put your account in risk and possibly allow them to get access to it. These things are very important to avoid properly, so I've listed some tips below.

Some scammers (although these aren't around much anymore due to an excellent game update) attempt to get you to say your pass.word by saying something like "OMG Did you know that Jagex blocks your pass.word if you say it backwards, see *******". Obviously, if you see someone doing this, you should report them in-game for scamming. While the chat filter actually does block you from saying your pass.word (and warns you in your chatbox), it doesn't block you from saying your pass.word backward, your bank pin, or your recovery questions.

Scammers, however, have recently updated their methods to attempt to get more, innocent players to fall for their tricks. Many now claim they are Jagex Moderators (often in a Funorb world), and that they will do something good for the player. This might include:

� Adding Membership to an account
� Adjusting an Account's stats
� Making the account a Moderator

If you see ANYONE claiming to be a Jagex Mod, but does not have a gold crown, report them for Staff Impersonation, add them to your ignore list, and ignore everything they have said. If they have given you a website URL, see Post 7.

Jagex will never ask for your account info EVER.

When you are ingame Jagex moderators will ALWAYS have a gold crown next to any messages they send to you or anything they say in the public chat. Also, Jagex moderators (with the exceptions of Andrew, Paul, and Ian) will have Mod at the start of their username.

Just remember, if its too good to be true, its probably a scam.

Post 7 - Scam Sites
___________________________________

Scam Sites are very dangerous. Usually, they are made by hijackers in an attempt to lure people to an official looking site, getting them to enter their account information, then using that info to steal the account.

There are a few easy ways to avoid this happening.


Never, never, NEVER enter your Runescape account information (username and pass.word) into any site other than Runescape, Funorb, and War of Legends. If the site address does not say "world-wide-web.runescape.(c)om/" (although Funorb and War of Legends would say world-wide-web.funorb.(c)om and world-wide-web.jagex.(c)om, respectivley), it is a scam site which you should leave immediately!

It is also important to note that some scam sites set up their URLs to look like the actual Runescape one, so it is important to note the "/" right after .(c)om, as a scam site might have a bit more after that just to trick you.

If you are ever given information to go to a scam site (such as someone saying "You can become a mod by going to =site address here=" ), report the player immediately for Website advertising.

In addition, if you have found a scam site, you should go and report it on the thread devoted to scam sites in Contact Us:

Report Scam Websites Here [v2]
Quick find code: 103-104-12-58898735

Also, if you do go to a scam site, and click a link on the site, you have the potential of getting a keylogger on your computer. Post 5 explains how to get rid of one.

Sometimes, you may come across an advertisment that links to a scam site or a real-world-trading site, or contains inappropriate material. If you ever see this kind of advert, you should report it to Jagex as soon as possible.

1) Click on the small question mark ('?') in the bottom-right corner of the advertisment. This will tell you the advert ID.

2) Go Help > Customer Support > Other > Advertising

Click the link at the top of the page to submit an advert complaint.

3) You will now be able to report the add. This will involve entering the advert ID you found earlier. If you were able to get the URL of the ad, enter that too. You will be asked for some other information, such as the appearance of the advertisment.

4) When you have completed and sent your report, Jagex will be able to have it removed.
(credit to The Zone)
_

Having your computer not fully updated with the latest security updates for your operating system is not a good thing when it comes to computer security.

These security updates fix flaws in the system, preventing a hijacker from getting in. At least once a week, you should go and download the newest updates from Microsoft, using the "Microsoft Update" feature. On a Mac, you should run "Software Update" at least once a week. (for most systems, the computer downloads and installs the updates automatically, but it is still a good thing to check).

One very useful tool, which Microsoft releases every month, is its "Malicious Software Removal" tool. It goes through and will find and remove any software harmful to the operating system. THIS IS NOT A SUBSTITUTE FOR A VIRUS SCANNER. A virus scanner is meant to remove all viruses, where the Malicious Software Removal tool is only designed to remove little, harmful programs which could jeopardize your computer's safety

Post 9 - I've been hacked, what should I do?
___________________________________

Mod Kat's thread at the top of this forum (QFC: 250-251-0-61111833) answers a lot of the questions commonly asked. This section is just to address some things that might not have been mentioned.

So, what should you do if you are hijacked?

First off, you should virus scan your computer immediately, just to make sure it was not a keylogger. The names of some free virus scanners are found in Post 5.

After the virus scan is complete, then you should change your Runescape pass.word, recovery questions, and if the hijacker added an e(c)mail on the account, remove it. If the hijacker added their own e(c)mail address on the account, here are step by step directions on how to remove it:

� Go to Account >> Account Management >> E(c)mail registration

� Login to the website as usual

� Leave the "Current Address" spot blank

� Enter your e(c)mail address in the "New Address" spot

� Enter the confirmation code sent to your e(c)mail.

After doing those steps, you will have to wait 14 days for the e(c)mail to change over. While the hijacker's e(c)mail address is sent an abort code, they shouldn't be able to enter it as long as your account is secure.

If you have consistent hijacking issues, and you don't have a keylogger on your account, you may need to have your account sent off to the Account Clean Team. While there isn't really a spot to request it, I would suggest posting over on Forum Emergencies (QFC: 103-104-17-61148852). That thread is where all serious issues should be reported, so a JMod there might be able to help you out.
